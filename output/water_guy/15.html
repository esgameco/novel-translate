The shattered silver coins rolled and clattered on the hard wooden bed, the sound becoming more and more captivating to hear.

It had been twenty-six days since he came into this world, almost a month, and he had finally saved up enough money to go learn martial arts, to see the most mysterious corner of the world.

It was not easy, too not easy.

Liang Qu kept rolling the shattered silver coins, gazing at their tempting bright luster, and for a moment he felt a resonance with Gao Langta's sentiments.

But now spending money was for the purpose of earning more in the future, to live a better life.

Liang Qu took a deep breath, separated out seven taels of the shattered silver, and carefully placed them in the small coin bag that Master Hu had kindly given him, tucking it safely in his bosom.

Yixing City was considered a big market, it wasn't just the ruffian Zhang Lantou causing trouble, among the thousands of people there were always some vile scoundrels.

But now he was no longer the helpless orphan, people would have to reckon with him to some extent.

The remaining two taels and three qian, along with one hundred and twenty-seven copper coins, were buried in the mud under the rice barrel, to be used for the autumn taxes.

The Great Shun had already implemented the "Tan Ding Ru Mu" and the "Yi Tiao Bian Fa" tax systems, the tax collection was very simple, just one item, but essentially it was no less, just merged together, the money had to be prepared, otherwise if you couldn't pay, you'd be taken to do corv√©e labor, undoubtedly a losing proposition.

After paying the taxes, when he entered the martial arts school, his finances would be much more abundant, first he would buy a mattress, this hard wooden bed was really uncomfortable to sleep on, and then save a few taels of silver to repay Uncle Chen, then his life would be much better.

Liang Qu was filled with an excited mood until he fell asleep in the late night.

The next morning, Liang Qu with slightly dark eye circles saw Li Libo, who also had dark eye circles.

The two looked at each other and burst out laughing.

"What time did you sleep last night?"

"Around the second watch, and you?"

"The fourth watch."

Liang Qu mocked, "No wonder your eye circles are darker than mine."

Li Libo snorted coldly, glancing around, and whispered, "Did you bring all the money?"

"Mm-hmm."

Liang Qu nodded, he had come out early this morning, just wanting to get to the martial arts school to pay the tuition as soon as possible, lest nightmares come true and some people develop improper thoughts.

After paying the tuition, no matter how bold the ruffians were, they wouldn't dare to go to the martial arts teachers and demand anything.

As for Li Libo, today was actually not the day he had planned to become a disciple, but seeing Liang Qu so anxious, he had to come earlier, so they could have each other's company, and Li's father had also given his consent.

Following the path, the two walked together to the entrance of Yixing City, spent ten copper coins to board a plank cart, and headed to the neighboring Pingyang Town.

The uneven road was lined with dense trees, and on a sunny and bright day, Liang Qu reckoned that few would dare to walk this night road, and he also had some anticipation for the Pingyang Town they were about to reach.

In Liang Qu's memory, he was like Chen Qingjiang, following his father to walk dozens of li to Pingyang Town to sell fish at a higher price.

That was a completely different scene from Yixing City.

"Fresh stir-fried chestnuts, fresh stir-fried chestnuts, fragrant and tender, sweet and delicious."

"Lamb blood soup, fresh lamb blood soup."

"Osmanthus flower buns, hot osmanthus flower buns."

The cries of the vendors filled the long street, which was also paved with bluestone, much wider than the streets in Yixing City, with the side alleys also paved, not just dirt roads.

The two sides of the long street were even more densely packed, the eaves of the shops connected, and the various stalls had set up colorful canopies outside the shops to compete for business.

"Sir, have two, they're so fragrant."

"Alright, give me two."

Liang Qu tossed over some copper coins and took two osmanthus flower buns, throwing one to Li Libo.

"Sizzle sizzle sizzle, hot hot hot." Li Libo juggled the bun with his hands.
