At Cloud Zecang's command, the Prime Minister of Cloud Lan hurriedly issued the order.

Although the people were bewildered and perplexed in their hearts, they of course dared not defy the King's command, not even with a million wills.

Subsequently, under the hurried instructions of the guards, the crowd quickly dispersed.

In no time, the East Main Gate of the capital city became deserted, with only a few lonely guards respectfully standing there.

Seeing this, Cloud Zecang continued, "Relay the order to the Imperial Guards to patrol the four sides of the East Main Gate, and no one is allowed to approach."

"Yes, Your Majesty."

At the same time, the news of King Cloud Zecang suddenly arriving at the East Main Gate with the officials spread like an epidemic throughout the capital.

The various factions in the capital were shaken, and they all turned their curious gazes towards the direction of the East Main Gate.

No one knew why the King had suddenly appeared at the East Main Gate with the officials.

If it were not for the order that no one was allowed to approach the East Main Gate, many people of power would have rushed there to pay their respects.

...

"Hmm? Father the King has appeared at the East Main Gate with the officials?"

At the same moment, in the palace, a young man dressed in luxurious brocade robes had a flashing gaze.

"Yes, Your Highness, the news is true, and the entire capital now knows about it," the subordinate reported with a bow.

Cloud Aochong's brows furrowed, and his eyes narrowed slightly. "Do you know the purpose of Father the King's actions?"

"Returning to Your Highness, no, I do not know." The subordinate was embarrassed, trembling as he spoke.

How could he know the King's intentions? It was a sudden development, and he was just a subordinate. No wonder His Highness, the Crown Prince, was not favored, to ask such an absurd question of his subordinate...He was truly foolish.

His Highness had always been rather self-righteous, always feeling that the King's favor towards him was just more subtle and not as obvious.

"Have the Second Princes shown any reaction after receiving the news?" Cloud Aochong continued to ask.

"Returning to Your Highness, the Second Princes have not shown any reaction," the subordinate respectfully replied.

Cloud Aochong suddenly stood up and walked towards the exit with his hands behind his back.

"Come, follow this Prince to the East Main Gate."

Ah?

The subordinate paused, then mustered up the courage to step behind Cloud Aochong, cautiously saying, "Your Highness, the King has ordered that no one is allowed to approach the East Main Gate..."

He was panicked. Didn't His Highness see that the other Princes didn't dare go to the East Main Gate? How could His Highness just...

"Hmm?" Cloud Aochong's steps paused, and he tilted his head, speaking indifferently, "Are you teaching this Prince how to act?"

The subordinate trembled, stammering, "I-I would not dare, it's just that, Your Highness, the King has given the order..."

As Cloud Aochong's trusted subordinate, he was naturally doing it for Cloud Aochong's own good.

If Cloud Aochong really went to the East Main Gate, he would definitely get scolded!

Cloud Aochong was about to get angry, but it seemed he also realized the subordinate was acting for his own good, so he waved his hand impatiently:

"Alright, no need to say more, this Prince has his own plans."

In Cloud Aochong's view, the fact that Father the King had suddenly appeared at the East Main Gate with the officials meant that there must be something going on.

And by ordering that no one was allowed to approach, it seemed this matter was quite serious, even urgent.

As the Crown Prince, of course he had to help Father the King resolve the difficulties!

Even if he couldn't be of much help, when Father the King saw him there, he would surely feel comforted and pleased.

Thinking of this, Cloud Aochong imagined Cloud Zecang's approving and praising gaze towards him, and his heart grew increasingly fervent.

He had truly surpassed the other Princes by a wide margin.

The subordinate saw this and secretly sighed, having no choice but to follow reluctantly.

He couldn't help but feel that something was not quite right...

Deeply hidden, deeply hidden!
