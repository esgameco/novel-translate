Moyu Town.

The Prefect's Mansion.

In the afternoon sunshine, Lu Changsheng sat in the cool pavilion in the backyard, holding a fishing rod and angling in the pond.

A young maid stood silently about ten feet behind him, carrying a tray of pastries and wine.

These past two days, Lu Changsheng had been eating and drinking well in this prefect's mansion. Chen Dadao had been considerate and didn't disturb him, even arranging a maid to serve his meals.

Quite perceptive.

"Pastries," Lu Changsheng said, eyes on the water.

But Lu Changsheng, as if he had eyes in the back of his head, softly said, "No need, I'll take it myself."

The maid's hand paused, and she trembled as she replied, "Yes, sir."
