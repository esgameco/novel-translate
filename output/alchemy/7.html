Chapter 7

Here is a full translation of the excerpt from Chinese to English:

Ever since he inherited this body through transmigration, his proficiency would only increase on the premise of success.

So he had never considered the possibility of gaining proficiency from failure.

But thinking carefully, why couldn't failure increase proficiency?

Success never came directly, but was accumulated through repeated failures.

Although the experience gained from failure was meager, wasn't that meager experience also a form of success?

It was like learning to drive. Beginners indeed couldn't take curves in drainage ditches.

But wasn't each successful uphill start, successful parallel parking, successful straight-line acceleration a small success in itself?

It was by accumulating these minor successes that one finally became an experienced city driver, a curve-mastering driver on Mountain Qiuming.

Moreover, there was one more point!

He wasn't without any complete success!

He had one!

The system achievement points helped him get started directly, which actually represented having the experience of one success.

Why could only sects and cultivator clans nurture orthodox pill furnacers, while wandering pill furnacers were so rare?

The reason was actually very simple - the initial investment was just too huge.

So huge that a pill furnacing apprentice, failing repeatedly, couldn't even see hope for one success. Wandering cultivators could only give up in the face of such enormous upfront investment.

But sects and clans were different - they had enough financial resources to support the repeated failures in the early stage.

And as long as the pill furnacing apprentice succeeded once, they would have the experience of one success, making it natural to replicate that success later.

This was why those who succeeded, succeeded forever.

And in fact, Luo Chen also had a backing force cultivating him, allowing him to skip over the entire investment stage of initial failures and get started directly!

And this system thing was even more formidable than sects and clans.

Some people were truly talentless, but as long as Luo Chen got started, he would be considered to have a certain talent.

Of course, this start relied on the achievement points provided by breaking through proficiency levels in each skill.

So to speak...

"All my successes come from my own efforts!"

Discovering his proficiency was rising, Luo Chen's mood, which had already adjusted quite well, became even better.

He was even howling excitedly like he had been injected with chicken blood!

However, having realized the principle of "more haste, less speed" yesterday, he also understood that this excited state was not very suitable for pill furnacing directly.

So today, he planned to do something else to adjust his state.

What?

Practice martial arts!

To be more precise, practice lightness skill.

Those close to him knew his situation - his cultivation base was not high, his spiritual power was not formidable, and the low-grade flying sword in his hands was just for show. He hadn't even learned the Guiding Technique, so the flying sword could only be used for slashing in his hands.

When the original host had entered the mountains back then, he had actually just been helping others transport demonic beast meat back to the city in the outermost area.

In this situation, what would give him the greatest ability to preserve his life?

The answer was naturally to be able to run!

In theory, the most suitable way for a qi condensation cultivator to run was with talisman transportation or riding a magical artifact for temporary flight, with the wind-summoning spell being the worst option.

But unfortunately, talisman transportation was extremely expensive, and ordinary people couldn't even buy it.

Magical artifacts required the Guiding Technique, and their effect was not even better than the wind-summoning spell.

As for the wind-summoning spell, a common item that Luo Chen couldn't afford before - a single copy cost dozens of low-grade spirit stones.

So he could only set his sights on the lightness skill of secular martial arts.

Although it was just a mortal-level martial art, it wasn't completely useless, at least not relying on spiritual power.

Luo Chen's spiritual power was not much to begin with.

The spiritual power of low-level scattered cultivators is actually not that great. In many cases, battles are just about standing still and outputting attacks.

So secular light skills that don't consume spiritual power still have their uses in martial arts!

Sitting at the main gate, he opened up a light skill secret manual that he had bought for ten taels of gold from the market street.

'Wandering at Ease'

What an awesome name!

The introduction was also awesome: "Riding the righteous forces of heaven and earth, commanding the subtleties of the six breaths, to wander endlessly!"

Of course, in reality it was just the great fantasy of the founder.

After carefully reading it several times, Luo Chen tried to put it into practice, no, put it into footwork!

This was not a set of bizarre footwork-based combat light skills, but rather travel light skills focused on borrowing force, deflecting force, adjusting one's form at high speeds, coordinating with rhythmic breathing to achieve fast and long-distance running.

When he first started practicing, Luo Chen felt a bit unaccustomed to it.

What was this borrowing force, deflecting force stuff? How do you command the six breaths?

But after running on the plain across the stream for the whole morning, he really did gain some insights.

"When the wind is strong, adjust your form and change directions, don't run against the wind."

"If there are branches or slopes, you can borrow the force to leap, and when landing either deflect the force for a light landing or put force into a rebound to accelerate your running."

"Your breathing rhythm can't be disordered, and your mindset must be calm, though not wandering at ease, you must still put in your full effort with sincerity."

"Hey, this light skill actually has some profound substance to it."

While eating lunch, Luo Chen glanced at the proficiency panel.

In the skills section, there was now clearly a [Wandering at Ease Beginner: 13/100].

"I've reached the beginner level, and the proficiency is rising quite quickly!"

"Cultivating immortality is like a turtle's crawl, but the martial path is like a soaring roc. I must have been a peerless martial genius all along!"

Holding the big bowl, Luo Chen laughed foolishly.

It's a pity the martial path can't grant longevity, otherwise he could show off as a martial sage.

In the afternoon he practiced Wandering at Ease for half a day more, and Luo Chen became increasingly adept at it, his proficiency rising swiftly.

When Luo Chen rested, he investigated the reasons and attributed it to his current physical strength.

Eating spirit rice and monster meat every day, circulating spiritual qi with the Long Spring Skill, even if he was a pig, he'd be a demonic pig.

With a stronger physique, wasn't it natural that his physical abilities would be stronger too?

It was just like those basketball superstars in the previous world - do you think they became stars just from skills? No, first they had to have bodies full of natural talent.

Even the so-called "talentless" superstars had better bodies than ordinary professional basketball players.

He currently had sufficient physical strength. Studying a body cultivation manual, his performance wasn't super-human, he was just developing the physical capabilities he should have.

In the evening, Luo Chen soaked in the one-meter deep stream, lowering his head to dig out a pile of mud.

Then, relying on his memory, he sculpted a mud figurine.

With phoenix eyes, silkworm eyebrows, a majestic and formidable appearance - even art college girls would have to praise his sculpting skills.

"Just you wait, Guan Er. Tonight I'll stick a beard on you and carve you a crescent moon blade!"

As he muttered, the sounds of beast roars came from the forest miles away on the plain.

Then several rays of light flashed out from the trees.

Luo Chen quickly stood up warily, went back into the house to retrieve his sword, and closed the main gate.

On the plain across the stream, those rays of light had already landed in the wild grass, gazing towards the depths of the distant mountains.
