Chapter 5

Here is a full translation of the Chinese excerpt to English:

The next day, very early before dawn, Luo Chen crawled out of bed.

Outside was a misty haze, and if you listened carefully, you could occasionally hear the roars of beasts from the vast mountains.

But those had nothing to do with him anymore.

Today, or from now on, he would be very busy.

First was sorting and processing all kinds of medicinal herbs.

Most of the herbs bought from the street vendor stalls were rather crudely processed, some even still not fully dried.

He first put those on a winnowing basket, to be taken out and sun-dried once the sun came up.

Then there were the jade lotus seeds.

He had to peel off the skins, only keeping the seed kernels, the skins had to be discarded.

Working by oil lamp light, Luo Chen peeled them one by one, until his eyes felt dry, before stopping.

"I wonder if this will cause nearsightedness?"

"But I'm cultivating immortality, surely I can't get nearsightedness!"

After finishing processing the big pile of lotus seeds, the sky had already brightened.

Soaking the lotus seeds in diluted chicken blood from a Fire Chicken, Luo Chen took out several winnowing baskets to sun-dry them.

Living on the outskirts of the outer city, though not very safe, had the relative advantage of a large usable area.

Normally when refining the Famine Dispelling Powder, there was one process that actually gave off a very pungent smell. Doing it in a crowded area would likely lead to complaints.

Old Daoist Chen was reported multiple times for tanning symbol papers at home, and had to bribe the officials with a few good talismans to smooth things over.

Luo Chen didn't have that concern.

The jade yam had to be diced into cubes, and some sliced into pieces - this portion was kept for refining the Famine Dispelling Powder.

Luo Chen wasn't the type to put all his eggs in one basket.

In the initial refinement of the Myriad Wonder Pill, he could foresee there would be many failures.

It was entirely possible for all of them to fail, resulting in a total loss!

After all, he was just starting out with beginner-level proficiency.

In this situation, he absolutely could not let his original income stream be cut off.

In the worst case of a total loss, at least he could still rely on the earnings from the Famine Dispelling Powder to sustain his livelihood, and then make a comeback.

After processing the jade yam, it was already noon.

It was time to eat!

Feeling the hunger in his stomach, Luo Chen smacked his lips.

It's said immortal cultivators don't eat mundane grains, but hunger still had to be endured!

Washing his hands, Luo Chen entered the kitchen.

He scooped out a small bowl of translucent spirit rice from the rice bin, and poured it directly into a small pot without rinsing.

This was spirit rice, a spirit stone could only buy ten pounds of it!

He couldn't bear to rinse it, fearing some of the spiritual energy would be washed away.

Going to a hidden compartment next to the kitchen, he pressed on it, and the ground opened up to reveal a small underground cellar about three feet square.

Hanging inside was half a side of beef!

A first-grade demonic beast, a yellow ox!

This stuff was also expensive, about one low-grade spirit stone for ten pounds.

The reason Luo Chen had hundreds of pounds of this meat wasn't because he was a glutton, but because yellow ox meat was the main ingredient in the Famine Dispelling Powder.

One pound of meat, plus an appropriate amount of ginseng, angelica and other medicinal ingredients, could produce five bottles of Famine Dispelling Powder, which sold for one spirit stone!

He always tried to buy more at once, as buying in bulk was cheaper, and he stored it with ice.

Looking at this half side of beef, Luo Chen's mouth watered a little.

Then with a kitchen knife, he sliced off a small two ounces of meat, and took out some regular vegetables from the ice cellar.

He washed the meat and vegetables thoroughly clean, diced the vegetables, sliced the meat into very thin pieces, and put everything into the small pot.

Add firewood!

Light the fire!

With both hands forming hand seals and murmuring incantations, after about two breaths, a small fireball appeared in his palm.

Go!

With a whoosh, the firewood in the stove immediately ignited.

"This time it went very smoothly, unlike that stove-exploding fireball incident when I first came through, that shouldn't happen again!"

Luo Chen smiled slightly, with a hint of self-satisfaction.

Glancing at the status panel, his proficiency in the Fireball Spell, which was at 268/300, had now jumped to 269/300!

Currently he had only cultivated three spells in total.

The water-based purification technique, the wood-based binding technique, the fire-based fireball technique.

Among these three spells, his proficiency was highest with the fireball technique.

This was not without reason.

Initially, he just found cultivating immortality mysterious and fun, so he would casually cast fireballs for amusement when bored.

He had experimented with the effects of this spell - slow speed, high energy consumption, but indeed great power!

As long as it hit the target, it would melt gold and iron, burn bones to ashes.

But the prerequisite was hitting the target!

For a normal cultivator to cast the fireball spell, from forming the hand seals to chanting the incantation, it would take at least three breaths.

Three breaths, and the enemy would have had enough time to kill him 180 times over.

At first, Luo Chen was about the same, and each cast would consume one-tenth of his total spiritual power.

It was then that he realized why low-level cultivators rarely used spells in combat.

They would either use magic weapons or talismans, both of which were faster, less draining, and not much weaker than spells.

Knowing this, he lost interest in low-level spells like the fireball technique.

However, as he used the fireball to start fires for each pill concoction, his proficiency improved, and he realized spells may have their uses in battle after all.

Like now, the energy cost was still one-tenth, but the casting time had been reduced to within two breaths.

And this was just at the proficient level.

What if he reached perfect mastery?

What if he reached grandmaster-level mastery?

So whenever the fireball could be used, he would almost always cast it once or twice.

Little by little, it adds up!

Amidst these random thoughts, the rice in the pot was almost done.

Smelling the aroma of the rice, Luo Chen took out a piece of pickled radish from the jar.

A big bowl of beef stew rice, full of spiritual energy, with an appetizing mix of red, green, yellow and white colors, paired with a few pieces of refreshingly sour and sweet pickled radish.

Sitting under the eaves, facing the cool stream breeze and sun, he ate heartily.

This was Luo Chen's most enjoyable time of the day!

If not for the pressures of survival and the pursuit of immortality, a meal like this every day would truly be blissful.

But alas, Luo Chen had no choice - he was a man with a system, one who would shine brightly even under the dark night sky!

"Damn, why does this thing stink so much!"

This destined shining man covered his chest and leaned over the stream, repeatedly gagging.

He didn't expect that the fire hound whip he bought from the Hundred Herb Hall would reek to that degree when undergoing special treatment.

Putting on a mask, Luo Chen mustered his courage and resumed processing the fire hound whip.

Flipping open the skin with one hand to apply the special liquid, he chanted to himself:

"It doesn't stink, it doesn't stink."

"It's a spirit stone, all spirit stones, a fragrant spirit stone!"

After finishing one fire hound whip, he rushed to the stream without delay.

"Urgh!"

Processing the raw materials was very laborious and time-consuming, but could not be done halfheartedly!

In fact, it was even more crucial than the concoction process itself.

It was simple logic - no matter how perfect the pill formula, how exquisite the technique, how top-notch the cauldron, if there were issues with the materials themselves, the end result was doomed.

It was like building a tall building - if the foundation was flawed, what was the point of discussing anything else?

After seven days, all the raw materials had undergone preliminary and advanced processing to reach the most perfect state possible.

Luo Chen had also taken the time to concoct a few batches of Hunger Dispelling Powder, forming them into pills and storing them away.

But he didn't plan to sell them this time.

He planned to wait until the All-Marvelous Pills were ready, then sell them together.

In the coming period, that iron cauldron containing traces of spirit ore would be cleaned for use in concocting the All-Marvelous Pills.

Standing before the stove, Luo Chen solemnly donned an apron.

Bowing to the blackened kitchen ceiling, "Guardian Deity Guan Er of my hometown, the Venerable Lord Lao Zi, the great Pill Saint of this land, bless me with a successful concoction!"

Light the cauldron!

Start the fire!

(End of chapter)
