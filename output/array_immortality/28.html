Mo Hua could only give up on the idea.

For example, the summer vacation that lasted over a month had begun.

A winter break and a summer break were the two longest vacations of the year.

The weather was scorching hot, and even demons didn't like to appear much. The demons that did appear were all fire-type, with stronger demonic powers in this weather, making them extremely difficult to deal with. So the summer vacation period was also the off-season for demon hunting.

Moxuan would stay home for a relatively long time, only occasionally going out for a few days when a team member discovered some valuable demons, then returning with some spirit stones or fur and such from the demons.

Once when Moxuan went out for a few days, he brought back a large bundle upon returning home. When Mohua opened it, she found it was a large bundle of beef tendon. She couldn't help but open her mouth wide, "Dad, this is..."

"This is wild ox meat. It doesn't have much spiritual energy and isn't worth much money, but compared to other demons, the meat quality isn't too bad and doesn't have too strong a smell."

"The demon hunting team didn't have much gain recently. We only got this wild ox. After skinning it and cutting the meat, we couldn't even sell it because everyone complained the beef was too tough and old, impossible to cook tender or chew. So we each took some and brought it back to eat." Moxuan explained.

Mohua said uncertainly, "Dad, are you going to cook this meat?"

Moxuan rubbed Mohua's head. "What? You don't think your dad's cooking is good?"

Mohua smiled without saying anything.

At noon, Moxuan stewed the beef. It took him a great effort to finally cook it tender, serving it in a large open white porcelain bowl.

"Try it and see how it tastes!"

The white porcelain bowl contained reddish-brown braised beef.

After seeing the beef, Liu Ruhua put down her chopsticks and only used a spoon to scoop up a mouthful of soup, slowly drinking it.

Mohua, however, picked up a piece of beef and put it in her mouth. After chewing a few times, her teeth felt sore, and the meat stubbornly refused to be chewed apart.

Mohua awkwardly but politely spit out the meat, also picking up a spoon to drink some soup, then praising, "The soup is not bad!"

Moxuan glared at him unhappily, "The soup is made from the sauce your mother prepared, of course it's good."

Liu Ruhua smiled faintly, "I know you mean well, but this dish is not something that can be made casually. Even using spiritual power to stoke the stove fire to cook things, there are quite a few points to pay attention to - when to use high heat, when to use low heat, when to add what sauce ingredients and how much...it takes skill."

Hearing this, Mohua suddenly asked, "So if it's cooked for a long enough time, could this beef be stewed soft?"

Liu Ruhua said, "In theory yes, but a cultivator would have to watch the pot day and night, constantly using spiritual power to stoke the stove fire..."

"An ordinary cultivator definitely couldn't do that, and any cultivator capable probably wouldn't be that bored," Moxuan also said.

"What about using a formation array?" Mohua's eyes lit up.

"A formation array..." Liu Ruhua thought for a moment, then said, "I'm not too sure about that. But I've heard the head chef at the restaurant say that some large restaurants have artifact refiners make special stoves, and then array masters draw formation arrays on them. That way, they only need spirit stones to power it, allowing long simmering of ingredients without expending a cultivator's spiritual power. However, hiring an array master is very expensive. At least the previous restaurant couldn't afford to build such a stove."

"I see..."

Mohua looked pensive.

Moxuan also picked up a piece of beef and put it in his mouth. After failing to chew it, he had to admit, "Indeed, it's not easy to chew." Then he just swallowed it whole, using his spiritual power to forcibly refine it.

That night, while practicing array formations on the remnant stele, Mohua was also thinking about the restaurant matter.

The next day, instead of drawing arrays at home, Mohua went out into the scorching sun towards North Street.

The sweltering heat made him sweat profusely after just a few steps. Nevertheless, there were still some small street vendors enduring the blazing sun, feebly hawking their wares.

Mohua arrived at North Street and found the largest restaurant there - the Fulu Restaurant. He asked the manager, "Is your young master surnamed An, called An Xiaopang...no, An Xiaofu?"

The manager didn't look down on Mohua for his ordinary attire. Instead, guessing from Mohua's familiar manner that he might truly be an acquaintance of the young master, he politely replied, "You're correct, young sir. The young master is upstairs. Shall I announce you?"

Mohua also replied courteously, "If you could please inform him that a fellow cultivator surnamed Mo wishes to see him on some matter."
