Inside the hall, the air began to grow restless and uneasy.

Among the disciples, Ma Da's martial arts skills were top-notch. He was an elder of the Zhen'an Magistracy. Niu Da had even cultivated inner powers and was at the middle stage of refining qi and condensing it into essence.

Two great masters, disappeared without a sound?

Yi Chen and Lin Bai Hu exchanged a glance, both understanding the meaning in each other's eyes.

"Something has happened."

Yi Chen abruptly stood up, taking the lead to prepare to go out and search, with Lin Bai Hu following closely half a step behind him.

Just then, a demonic wind suddenly arose. The lanterns in the hall were instantly extinguished, and the main door slammed shut with a loud bang.

Only the still burning charcoal fire remained the sole source of light inside.

A rather bold junior disciple went forward and tried forcefully pulling on the door handle a few times, but the door remained motionless, as if someone had cast it with molten iron from the outside.

"Master, what should we do now?" the disciple asked Yi Chen.

Somehow, Yi Chen had become the decision maker, the core leader of this group.

"What to do?"

"Bring it on!"

"Get out of my way."

"These trickster things don't even regard me at all."

"They dare to mess with those under my protection."

A fierce grin appeared on Yi Chen's face. With a forceful thrust of his legs that swelled up hugely, he delivered a powerful kick straight into the gap between the two door panels.

The doors flew apart as if struck by a cannon shell, exploding into powder mid-air.

Amidst the stunned expressions of the others, Yi Chen strode over the threshold and went forth.

"Everyone follow me, search room by room, don't leave out even the cellars."

Yi Chen's shout pierced the silent night air.

Hearing this, the disciples felt their courage swell, their backs straightening as they hurried to follow.

What they feared was not "everyone follow me", since the leader had already taken the lead to charge. What would they be afraid of?

What they feared was "everyone come at me", with the leader watching from behind.

Like the classic scene in Journey to the West where the Nine-Headed Snake tells Friar Sand to go and get rid of the Tang Monk's party...who wouldn't be scared in that situation?

But was Yi Chen afraid at this moment?

He really wasn't too afraid.

That demonic thing lurking in the dark dared only to make small troublesome moves, unlikely to be able to do much to all of them.

Otherwise, why bother with such complications? It could have just directly overwhelmed their group.

Not only corpses, but incidents too, can speak for themselves.

With the demonic thing acting weak, Yi Chen grew bold.

What a leader should do in such a time is to gather everyone together, to avoid being picked off one by one.

What should absolutely not be done is to split up forces.

Knowing things are dynamically changing, one must view problems with a developmental perspective.

If at this point he still hesitated and considered sacrificing others to fill the breach, then he would not be a wise man, but a liability to the enemy.

That would just be feeding it.

Soon, the entire manor became lively as Yi Chen kicked open door after door, the disciples rummaging through like soldiers pillaging a village, upturning everything, even stabbing under beds with knives.

No one.

No one.

No one.

Not a single person, as if everyone in the entire manor had vanished, not even a servant could be seen.

Just then, a group of seven or eight people appeared from around the corner on the left, including the maid Yue Ya who had previously clashed with Yi Chen.

Leading them were a man and woman in white, the Taoist cultivators they had seen performing fire rituals earlier in the day.

Lin Bai Hu stepped forward to confer with the white-robed leader, and the two groups began marching in distinct yet orderly formations.

For some reason, a faint sense of unease began to rise in Yi Chen's heart.

After searching the kitchen, as the group prepared to move to the next location, the unease in Yi Chen's heart grew increasingly dense.

No, something wasn't right.

It seemed he had forgotten something.

He had the feeling he had overlooked some details.

Yi Chen's gaze fell on an osmanthus tree ahead, its trunk missing a large patch of bark that had been scraped off.

At this moment, alarm bells rang loudly in Yi Chen's mind. He abruptly raised his hand, signaling the group to halt, and turned to look back at those behind.

"Taoist friends performing the fire rituals, where is your disciple sister?"
