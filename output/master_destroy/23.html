Taking advantage of the light provided by the lantern penetrating through the fog, a white-haired old man pushing a small crimson handcart came over, while continuously calling out in a low voice: "Selling knives, selling knives."

"Damn it, you gave me a fright." Wang Dazhu rushed forward and kicked the white-haired old man's cart with a clang, "Old guy, instead of sleeping at this late hour, you're out selling your old mother."

At every city gate of Fengyun County, there were bagua mirrors for warding off evil spirits, so generally evil forces couldn't enter. Additionally, as the city had enjoyed a long period of peace, when Wang Dazhu saw that it was just an old man who emerged from the thin mist, he immediately put his mind at ease.

There couldn't be that many evil spirits, and it wasn't like they were outside the city walls, he had simply overthought it.

The old man was probably just a bit mentally ill, a situation Wang Dazhu had encountered before - he had even seen people streaking naked late at night.

He kept kicking the white-haired old man's small cart, displaying his valor, as if the fearful person from before had nothing to do with him.

Wang Seven and Wang Eight naturally followed closely behind.

After all, if the leader had kicked it, and you just stood there watching, wouldn't that make it seem like you weren't on the same wavelength as the leader?

What surprised him a bit was that although the cart looked dilapidated, it was surprisingly sturdy on the inside. Despite his several kicks, he didn't manage to kick the cart apart.

"Sir, would you like to buy a knife? Very cheap," the white-haired old man said faintly, looking at the three men's actions without getting angry.

The lantern light shone on the old man's face, highlighting his eerie smile, which looked extremely creepy at night.

"Buy your old mother, get lost! That rusty knife of yours can barely kill a chicken, who knows if you dug it out from some graveyard. Scram, scram, get lost quickly!" After venting for a while, when Wang Dazhu saw the old man's eerie smile, he couldn't help feeling a chill down his spine, so he mustered his courage and yelled.

"My knives are very sharp, they can easily cut through bones and people," the white-haired old man remained unperturbed upon hearing this. He picked up a rusty machete from the cart, and with one swing, he chopped off his own head, which rolled to Wang Dazhu's feet.

The severed head's mouth kept opening and closing on the ground: "Sir, is my knife sharp or not? Just one hundred years of your life, or you can trade me your livers instead, I want your livers."

The lantern went out again.

This bizarre turn of events left Wang Dazhu and the other two dumbfounded. They wanted to run, but their legs seemed rooted to the ground, unable to move.

They wanted to open their mouths and shout, but their voices got stuck in their throats, unable to come out.

A look of intense despair appeared in Wang Dazhu's eyes. If he could see himself in a mirror at this moment, he would realize that his expression was the same as the old village chief of Li Family Village when Wang Dazhu had broken his limbs.

A pungent odor spread through the air, and the ground became wet and slimy.

Although Wang Dazhu appeared fierce and often fancied himself a ruthless man, when disaster befell him, it exposed his true weak nature.

The white-haired man's body walked over to the severed head, dusted off the dirt, and directly reattached it to the neck.

Realizing he had put it on backwards, he used both hands to twist the head 180 degrees.

The foul stench in the air became even thicker.

"Sirs, would you like to buy a knife? I told you my knives are sharp," the white-haired man said gloomily as he approached the three men.

"Since you're not saying anything, that means you agree. I want your livers."

Wang Dazhu and the other two struggled desperately, but it was as if an invisible force was weighing them down, rendering them unable to speak or move.

They shook their bodies frantically, wanting to cry out: "I don't want it, I don't want your broken knife, I want my liver!"

But the white-haired man paid them no heed, taking out that rusty machete to start cutting them open.

Blood flowed everywhere.

Soon, Wang Dazhu and the other two were disemboweled, their livers and guts exposed.

Wang Dazhu suffered the most, as his iron abdominal muscle made him tougher, requiring a few extra slashes to disembowel him, adding to his torment.

The white-haired old man produced a gray cloth bag from somewhere, as if performing a magic trick, and extended both hands to pluck out each of the three men's livers, placing them one by one into the bag.

As he did so, he muttered, "Your livers are a bit spoiled, not good quality, what a loss, a big loss. If I had known earlier..."
