"What Lin Baihu said makes sense. With so many of us here, even if Lanxiu Building is a den of tigers and dragons, we have to level it today."

Yi Chen agreed with Lin Baihu's words without hesitation, but his feet remained motionless, as if they had taken root.

He wasn't an idiot. Just smashing the main gate and slashing those white lanterns would be enough. Did he really have to charge in first?

How much money did he even have?

At present, he was at most a temporary outsourced worker. The officially registered worker Lin Baihu was right beside him. If he still rushed in first, then it would be like his brain had turned into muscle.

Throwing bricks from outside, smashing the door and lanterns - basically there wouldn't be any major trouble. But being the first to charge inside, that really could mean walking into an ambush.

No way, no way.

Yi Chen's mind was like a clear mirror, his calculations clicking loudly.

After all, it wasn't like his fellow disciples were trapped inside. He wasn't in a hurry.

Lin Baihu turned his gaze to Master Lianhua. Lianhua looked up at the sky, studying the clouds.

Lin Baihu's gaze shifted to Daoist Qingxu, who had already closed his eyes and begun chanting sutras.

Lin Baihu looked at Shitou Yueyue. Her water-cutting eyes also glanced at Lin Baihu, but she said nothing.

"......"

"The atmosphere in Fengxun County is really poor."

"When they agreed, each one was more eager than the last. But when it comes time to act, not a single one is easy to fool - even that seemingly reckless Monk Yi Cheng has hundreds of calculations."

Lin Baihu inwardly sighed. With no other choice among the group of cultivators, he took the lead himself.

He strode ahead, while Yi Chen and the others closely followed behind. Together they entered the mist-shrouded front hall of Lanxiu Building.

As soon as they entered, Yi Chen couldn't help but furrow his brow.

The hall was covered in a pool of blood and gore, the pungent stench of blood wafting through the air.

Yet not a single corpse could be seen.

The hall was in utter disarray, a complete mess.

He looked ahead, and on the front wall, a huge bluestone brick had pierced through the chest of a paper figure with rouge painted on its cheeks, nailing it firmly to the wall.

That paper figure seemed almost alive, its hands still flailing as it clutched the long bluestone brick in its chest, as if trying to pull itself out from the wall.

No, it was trying to pull itself out. Yi Chen's eyes narrowed - although slowly, the paper figure on the wall had already pulled itself halfway out.

"What a pitiful little thing."

Yi Chen was kindhearted and couldn't bear to see evil spirits suffer. So he decided to help it out.

After all, the way those paper arms and legs were flailing looked so miserable.

He drew his Dragon Slaying Sword and sliced off all the figure's limbs, chopping it into pieces.

There, that was better. The paper figure didn't have to struggle so painfully anymore.

How nice.

The figure on the wall went still, its crimson eyes dyed with some unknown pigment staring blankly at Yi Chen.

Staring unmoving.

If Yi Chen guessed correctly, that long bluestone brick was one he had thrown, accidentally nailing this patrolling paper figure to the wall in the mist.

"Monk Yi Cheng, what is the meaning of this?" Seeing this scene, Shitou Yueyue's brow furrowed as she couldn't help questioning Yi Chen - his behavior was indeed a bit deranged.

However, Yi Chen did not immediately respond. Instead, he thrust his sword into the paper figure's chest. Its body violently shuddered, and a wisp of flame slowly ignited from the point where the Dragon Slaying Sword met its body, emitting plumes of black smoke.

After observing for two breaths, another sword gleam flashed, and the figure's head dropped to the floor while its body hung burning on the wall, consumed in a burst of flames.

Yi Chen noticed that his Finger of Judgment showed no increase in its deep red value, indicating this paper figure was likely a crafted artifact.

"Is the Daoist trying to test the weaknesses of this paper man?"

The Lotus Dharma Master, who had been observing Yi Chen for a while, finally figured out the reason and asked.

"Compassionate master."

"The Dharma Master's insight is impeccable. I indeed had the intention to probe the weaknesses of this ghostly thing."

Yi Chen pondered for a moment before explaining the purpose behind his earlier actions.

"This paper man possesses considerable strength. I noticed that even though I pinned it to the wall with long green bricks, by the time we entered, this ghostly thing had already pulled itself out halfway."

"Earlier, I tried to cut off its four limbs, but apart from restricting its movement, the paper man did not suffer much damage, indicating its vulnerability does not lie there."

"Next, I pierced this paper man's chest multiple times with my Pure Yang sword. Even when I infused a trace of my Pure Yang inner force into the blade, the paper man only had its surface slightly burned, but still did not sustain major damage."

"It was not until I severed its head that this paper man spontaneously combusted."

The Pure Void Daoist furrowed his brow upon hearing this.

"Do you mean, Daoist, that apart from its head, this paper man has virtually no vulnerability, and is also highly resistant to our mystical arts?"

At this point, everyone could not help but turn their gaze towards Yi Chen, secretly admiring him.

If Yi Chen had not discovered the characteristics of this paper man in advance, they would have suffered a huge loss caught off guard.

Lin Bai Hu finally showed a smile. As the saying goes, a man of great reputation is no mere braggart. This Yi Cheng Zi Daoist's skills were indeed formidable, and his mind truly meticulous.

He cleared his throat and boosted everyone's morale:

"You are all exceptionally talented individuals. With your assistance on this journey, our chances of success have increased."

"It seems the evil forces within this building are merely small fry."

"The mastermind behind this is inept. If I were the mastermind, I would have hidden a squad of paper man ambushers, armed to the teeth, taking advantage of the fog to launch a surprise attack. Wouldn't that be marvelous?"

"Hahaha...huh?"

Suddenly, Lin Bai Hu's smile froze on his face.

The fog in the main hall began to churn like cold water added to a pot of hot oil.

The rustling sounds of something approaching grew louder and louder.

"Everyone be careful."

The Lotus Dharma Master warned in a low voice.

Each person took out their respective weapons and made preparations.

The Water Moon Master, Lotus Dharma Master, and Pure Void Daoist remained the same trio as before.

Among the three, the Water Moon Master and Lotus Dharma Master seemed to be at the middle stage of Refining Vitality into Energy, while the Pure Void Daoist was a bit stronger, probably nearing the late stage.

Lin Bai Hu's magic weapon was a hundred-times refined battle blade, engraved with tiny seal scripts as small as mosquitoes. He pondered for a moment, then took out a fiery red talisman from his chest and painfully slapped it onto the blade.

The talisman spontaneously combusted, and in an instant, a red glow slowly appeared on Lin Bai Hu's long blade, spreading from the tip until it reached the guard before stopping.

This wondrous sight even made Yi Chen look twice in admiration.

The dog officials of the Zhen'an Magistracy sure had many tricks up their sleeves.

No sooner said than done.

As the strange objects rapidly approached from afar, the fog could no longer conceal their forms - two squads of heavily armored, towering paper men charged at them, some wielding battle blades, some with chain hammers, some with bronze staves, moving as swiftly as the wind.

Yi Chen and the others deeply looked at Lin Bai Hu, whose smile was frozen on his face.

One of them opened their mouth wide, but none of them would say who it was.
