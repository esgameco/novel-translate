The fourth day.

The weather was a bit overcast today.

Yi Chen walked out of the separate courtyard, twisted his neck, stretched out in a big yawn, his bones cracking loudly.

After killing the demon insect last night, he rested for a bit before throwing himself back into investigating the great enterprise, only just finishing the full investigation now.

"Hey, where's little Meow?"

"This mutt hasn't followed me around for the past couple days, where has he been fooling around?" Yi Chen looked around but couldn't find his beloved pet.

At this moment, in the distant street appeared a strange black cat with wings growing from its ribs. It strode with elegant cat steps in the morning breeze.

Appearing with it were three slightly smaller female cats - a raccoon cat, a ragdoll, and a Persian cat.

They ran together in the morning wind, chasing each other playfully.

There was a beauty of "you didn't come at the right time, no, you came at precisely the right time."

Seeing this scene, Yi Chen couldn't help clenching his fists.

"Unfilial son!"

"He's only been born a few months and he's already such a playboy - is this the fierce azure beast of my Yinlong Temple? Useless, useless."

Seeming to notice Yi Chen's gaze, little Meow suddenly let out a ferocious meow towards the raccoon, ragdoll and Persian cats, fully displaying his cad nature. Then he tucked his tail and flew rapidly towards Yi Chen.

In a moment he leapt onto Yi Chen's shoulder, giving an ingratiating smile.

Yi Chen flicked little Meow's head unhappily, grabbed him by the scruff of the neck with one big hand and set him down on the ground.

He looked at the three pitiful female cats gazing this way from afar, not daring to come closer, and sighed helplessly.

"Meow, scram."

"Don't go too far, don't blame your dad if you get caught and stewed."

"Meow meow." (Okay, geez.)

With a slightly obsequious meow, little Meow turned into a black streak of lightning, running off to play again.

The pink paw pads touching the street weren't kicking up dust, but the remnants of his departed youth.
