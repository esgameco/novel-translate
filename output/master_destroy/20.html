The next day, the sun shone brightly.

Not far from Yinlong Temple, there was a place called Zhualong Pond, with deep waters of unfathomable depth.

A mountain spring cascaded down from over twenty meters high, splashing up water sprays taller than a person.

At this moment, a tall, bare-chested man was holding a large millstone, standing upstream of the cascading spring water, taking deep breaths repeatedly.

Watching the gushing mountain stream crash onto the deep pond surface, Yi Chen, standing at the high ground, felt a chill run down his spine.

However, he had no other choice. The red mark on his chest was like the Sword of Damocles hanging over his head, constantly reminding him that if he didn't get stronger quickly, he would be waiting for death.

"Damn it, if you're not ruthless, you can't stand firm. I'm going all out."

Yi Chen gritted his teeth, hugged the millstone, and leapt into Zhualong Pond.

People are driven to extremes.

This statement holds true whether viewed from a physiological or philosophical perspective.

If it were the past, he would never have subjected himself to such a brutal ordeal. Prisoner workouts and Ironman triathlons were already the limits of his efforts. But now, that was not enough.

He needed strength; he had an insatiable thirst for becoming stronger.

After going through two enhancements of the Pure Yang skill, he could now endure such an attempt without killing himself.

Boom!

Yi Chen leaped down, crashing heavily onto the deep pond surface, sending up sprays of water several meters high.

Immediately after, while hugging the millstone, he sank straight down to the bottom of the pond.

The immense water pressure constantly challenged the limits of his physiology. The parts of his body that made contact with the water surface upon jumping down were in excruciating pain, and his lungs were burning.

The faint purple pure yang inner qi within his body was circulating rapidly, swelling his muscles to resist the surrounding pressure.

He then hugged the millstone and slowly did squats on the pond bottom...

Time passed slowly.

For Yi Chen, each second felt like a year at the bottom of the pond.

One second.

Ten seconds.

One minute.

Three minutes.

Five minutes.

Despite his astonishing physical prowess, under such inhumane torment, after five minutes, Yi Chen could no longer endure. He kicked off, carrying the millstone, and burst out of the water surface.
