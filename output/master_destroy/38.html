Night time.

The moonlight was like water, but it wasn't just the moonlight that was like water - the ladies of the Lanxiu Pavilion were too.

As the famous brothel of Fengyun County, the Lanxiu Pavilion was naturally bustling with people every day. Especially after the government announced that the demonic aura of the Wang family had been eliminated, tonight the Lanxiu Pavilion welcomed a retaliatory surge of consumption.

After all, tightly wound nerves needed to be relieved.

And certain activities were the best remedy.

The middle-aged Li Fugui, the young master Li, was a prime example. He couldn't go a day without women.

Reading books was too tiring, martial arts training too bitter, and cultivating the Dao was out of reach. Only that one thing could make him feel alive.

The Li family had countless fields, farms, and shops. When his great-grandfather retired back home, he had already completed his life's work. What was wrong with Li enjoying himself?

He had tasted 200 taels per pound of tea before.

Moreover, a joyous event had occurred just the other day - his archenemy, the eldest young master of the Wang family, and his whole clan had ascended to heaven. From now on, no one would compete with him for the top beauties.

Henceforth, he would be the most dashing man at the Lanxiu Pavilion of Fengyun County.

Such a heartening event, how could he not come out and relax?

The moonlight like water shone on the welcoming ladies outside the Lanxiu Pavilion, accentuating their fair, alluring skin.

Facing the bustling crowd of guests, the ladies did not put on airs as often depicted in shows and films. Rather, they were exceptionally reserved, merely giving a curtsy to welcome the entering guests.

Vulgar lines like "Come play, sir?" or "Young Master Li, it's been so long, have you forgotten little Peach?" would never come from the mouths of the Lanxiu ladies.

Too low-class, too uncouth.

That's exactly what Li Fugui liked about the Lanxiu Pavilion.

High-class.

Those gaudy places like the Courtesan House or Lirichun Nunnery with their red-sleeved solicitations all over - his status and standing would be mocked if he went to such places and mingled with the riffraff. Wouldn't that make a mockery of his ancestors' efforts?

Three generations of his family's hard work could not be disgraced by going to such shameful places.

Amidst the lively crowd, Li Fugui and his gang of cronies laughed and joked as they entered the Lanxiu Pavilion.

With their airs, they naturally wouldn't squeeze into the first floor lobby to drink and make merry. The group, with Li Fugui the young master at the center, proceeded to a private room on the second floor.

After they were all seated, a bevy of beauties streamed into the room to attend to them. Drinking games commenced, occasionally punctuated by sips of strong liquor. It was quite a lively scene.

After three rounds of drinks, Li Fugui the young master suddenly had a whim. Embracing the beauty beside him, he posed a question to the group:

"Gentlemen, tell me, if we didn't rely on our ancestors' legacies, how could we become wealthy and noble?"

His words were met with silence in the room.

Clearly the young master's query had stumped his cronies.

"Master Wu, why don't you speak up? How did your ancestors make their fortune?" Li Fugui's gaze focused on his new lackey - the great young master of the Wu family from Qingyuan Town.

The great young master of the Wu family, or more accurately the current patriarch, for just over a month ago his father Wu Changshou had died of a stroke. The great young master Wu Qingxi wept tears of joy and swiftly took over his father's position, expelling his own younger brother.

Since the Wu family's fur and sundry goods business relied heavily on the Li family's connections, after becoming patriarch, Wu Qingxi had been fawning over the young master Li, constantly accompanying him in eating, drinking and playing in hopes of maintaining or even expanding his family's business.

Seeing the young master's question, Wu Qingxi looked troubled, unsure how to respond. Noticing the mocking looks from the other lackeys around, he suddenly had an epiphany and replied:

"My grandfather told my father and me that in this age, to become wealthy and noble, one relies solely on three things."

"What three things?" The young master Li and the other lackeys leaned in with interest.

"Those three are:"

"Seeing profit, forgetting righteousness. Two-faced treachery. Taking care of your sister-in-law."

"Haha, Master Wu is truly ingenious, and old Master Wu even more so - a true straight shooter!" The young master Li guffawed heartily, and a joyous atmosphere filled the room.

Seeing profit and forgetting righteousness, two-faced treachery - these were commonplace in their circle. The young master wouldn't look down on Master Wu for it.

With so many flatterers surrounding him, Master Wu's words instead amused him, like discovering an interesting new plaything.

The young master liked clever people, and obedient ones even more. Today, by denigrating his own family history and humbling himself before the crowd, Wu had pleased him. He could consider taking him in as a dog.

Especially that last line about caring for one's sister-in-law - brilliant, it reminded him of his cursed elder brother.

Tsk tsk.

He was starting to get into the mood.
