Here is the full English translation of the Chinese excerpt:

Morning.

Li Yan is about to leave.

Many people are seeing him off.

Among them is an acquaintance of Yu Xiao's.

Wang the Manager, the outer gate steward of Qinglan Valley.

He has come to see him off, and besides their personal relationship, there are other reasons. One can see his attendant handing over two items.

One is a blue-green robe.

One is a blue-green wooden plaque.

Manager Wang announces: "During his time in Baiyun Pavilion, Li Yan has made many contributions to the sect, and is now granted the status of an outer disciple to retire back home!"

Before leaving, Li Yan had given his real estate to Wei Biao, the Ascending Immortal Talisman to Yu Xiao, and donated all his remaining personal property without compensation to Qinglan Valley, after retrieving the assets from the Golden Knife Gang that had harmed his disciples.

He had been in Baiyun Pavilion for over twenty years.

He had already indirectly made many contributions to Qinglan Valley.

Although he was unable to become a formal disciple of Qinglan Valley, by donating all his personal assets in exchange for the status of an outer disciple, he was probably seeking some solace.

"Thank you, senior brother!"

Li Yan bowed to Manager Wang.

He then bowed three times towards Qinglan Valley.

Yu Xiao and Wei Biao stepped forward and put the blue robe on him.

This robe was similar in style to the one worn by Manager Wang, just with slightly simpler patterns, and made of ordinary fabric, without any special features.

Manager Wang said, "The spirit boat is about to set sail, Brother Li should board it quickly to avoid missing the departure time."

"Yes!"

Under the sendoff of the crowd, Li Yan boarded the flying boat. As he watched the boat gradually move away from Baiyun Pavilion, he gently stroked the plain and simple blue robe on him, and couldn't help but sigh wistfully.

"What was my life all about, after all?"

Yu Xiao watched Li Yan depart.

He knew that from now on, there would be no more Li Yan in Baiyun Pavilion, no more Li Yan in the cultivation world.

Perhaps.

Not long after.

In some unknown corner of this vast land, there would be a grave for the returning wanderer, but his experiences would not be celebrated, for this was just the mundane story of a failed cultivator.

Regardless of the world or industry, the focus is always on the movers and shakers at the forefront.

However, beneath the feet of every glorious figure, there are countless Li Yans who have exhausted themselves in pursuit, ultimately achieving nothing, and having to bow out in mediocrity and regret.

Yu Xiao still holds Li Yan and such people in deep respect.

Li Yan may have been just an insignificant and ordinary drop in the cultivation world, but it is precisely the countless such drops that come together to form the mighty rivers and oceans, allowing the most brilliant and dazzling waves to emerge.

"Master Li! Safe journey!"

Yu Xiao cupped his hands and bowed towards the direction the flying boat disappeared.

Although Li Yan's departure saddened and troubled him, it did not affect his state of mind.

He adjusted his mindset and began to continue his cultivation.

He first took out twenty spirit stones and bought forty spirit herb pills in one go, then applied for a long leave at the mine.

Manager Wang had no objections.

After all, he had already agreed to Li Yan's request to allow Yu Xiao to work freely without attendance constraints, and although Li Yan was now gone, this bond of gratitude remained.

Moreover, as Li Yan's direct disciple, Yu Xiao had already become a Blood Warrior, and would most likely be able to enter the Qi Refining stage in the future, making it unlikely for him to remain a miner forever.

After making the necessary preparations, Yu Xiao began his breakthrough cultivation directly in the Fist Pavilion.

After Li Yan's departure, the Fist Pavilion's owner became Wei Biao.

As Wei Biao's only true disciple, Yu Xiao naturally had the right to use the facilities here, and choosing to cultivate here was both convenient for seeking guidance and relatively safer.

In the blink of an eye, a month had passed.

To focus and avoid distractions, Yu Xiao had not gone to the Great Scenery even once, spending most of his time cultivating the Azure Wood Technique, and only occasionally helping Wei Biao manage the Fist Pavilion and seeking his advice.

After a month of cultivation, his progress in the blood qi aspect was not significant, but at least there was no regression.

The cultivation of the Azure Wood Technique showed remarkable results, as he had spent dozens of spirit stones, which was a considerable amount for any wandering cultivator.
