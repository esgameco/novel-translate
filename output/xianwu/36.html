Da Niu Yu was brought in.

He looked a bit sturdier than half a month ago.

Overall, he still appeared dark-skinned and thin.

This appearance didn't quite match his name.

And it was completely impossible to tell he was a one-in-a-million martial arts prodigy!

"Greetings, adoptive father!" Da Niu Yu was very excited.

The adoptive father was the idol and hero in the hearts of every child in the institute.

Half a month ago, he single-handedly wiped out the bandits of Stone Tiger Fortress. This event was heavily publicized in the institute.

It had a huge impact on the young hearts of the children!

How did the cultured and refined adoptive father manage to take down hundreds of fierce and vicious bandits? Could it be that he knew magic?

The already towering image of the adoptive father now seemed godlike!

"Don't be nervous, let me take a look."

Xiao Yu placed his hand to sense Da Niu Yu's pulse. He felt streams of martial artist's qi flowing smoothly through the body. This was indeed a sign that all the meridians had been opened.

With qi reinforcing the body, physical constitution would greatly increase.

Even though Da Niu Yu was only ten years old and hadn't fully developed yet, if he were to arm wrestle with adult men, probably few ordinary people could be his match.

"Good, even faster than expected. It seems you've really been working hard!"

Da Niu Yu was overjoyed.

He quickly said: "It's not hard!"

These days, he would get up to practice martial arts before the rooster crowed every morning. And at night when all the other children were asleep, he would still practice alone in the dark, all for the sake of earning his adoptive father's praise and recognition again!

Xiao Yu was naturally very gratified.

He had randomly adopted a group of orphaned children.

And unexpectedly picked up such a fine uncut jade.

With Da Niu Yu's aptitude,

As long as he worked hard enough and with proper guidance,

He would likely be able to reach the level of first-rank ancient martial arts master in the future!

And if he had sufficient resources and lucky encounters, it wasn't impossible for him to enter the realm of grandmasters.

What does it mean to be a one-in-a-million martial arts prodigy? This was a true one-in-a-million martial arts prodigy!

Xiao Yu's own mediocre skills weren't worth mentioning.

But this was truly grandmaster potential!

The Great Jing Empire had a population of 800 million.

There were only about ten grandmasters.

Such beings had already reached the pinnacle of martial arts, and could be counted on one's fingers in the martial world of every era.

If Xiao Yu could nurture such an impressive martial artist, it would greatly boost his reputation and prestige!

In the future, whether Da Niu Yu roamed the martial world or joined an organization like the Great Xia Society to make a career, all his accomplishments could be attributed in part to Xiao Yu.

"Starting today, you'll come here to practice martial arts. I'll personally teach you fist techniques and martial arts."

"Thank you, adoptive father!"

Although Xiao Yu had practiced martial arts for less than a year, he had already become quite proficient in the Spring Thunder Fist. He had also often observed Wei Biao teaching disciples in the martial arts hall, so he was confident in teaching the Spring Thunder Fist to Da Niu Yu.

Of course,

Since he was going to teach the fist technique,

He might as well teach more people.

Among the children, there were others with good physiques who showed potential for martial arts. Even if they hadn't reached Da Niu Yu's level, they could also be called to listen in. The guards, and even the maids and servants could learn if they wanted to.

The several masters were very surprised.

The Spring Thunder Fist sounded ordinary,

But it was definitely a top-tier grandmaster-level fist technique.

Such techniques were invariably closely guarded secrets of various schools and sects. Yet they, who were just private tutors and training partners hired by Young Master Yu, were actually allowed to learn such a high-level martial art. This was definitely a stroke of luck.

Did this mean

That Young Master Yu regarded them as his own people?

Perhaps he was planning to groom them as his trusted aides!

When they realized this, the masters became even more excited.

Two weeks later.

Da Niu Yu had mastered the 36 forms of the practice method.

Xiao Yu's life was still quite leisurely. On one hand, he was steadily cultivating with the goal of reaching the sixth rank. On the other hand, he was setting up businesses and factories and recruiting people in the Great Jing Empire. He also spent quite a bit of time practicing minor spells in the immortal cultivation realm.

For him,

Almost thirty days had passed in total.

The late rice in Mei County was starting to be harvested.

The 100 mu of spirit rice paddy had indeed yielded a bumper harvest.

Each mu was estimated to produce an average of 800 jin of unhusked rice.

Xiao Yu did a rough calculation. The total of 80,000 jin of unhusked rice, with a milling rate of about 70%, would produce 56,000 jin of spirit rice.

Even if the quality was inferior to the White Cloud Workshop's spirit rice,

It was still a rare and unheard-of good rice in the Great Jing Empire.

If this batch of rice were transported back to White Cloud Workshop to be sold...
