Yesser led a group of people deep into the mainland and found a saltwater lake located inland. This place had nothing, but it had a vast expanse of water with white waves rippling across its surface. Salt accumulated on the shores like patches of white clouds. There must have been a volcano underneath the lake bottom, but it had long been extinguished.

"Your Majesty!"

"This place is quite similar to the one you were looking for. Go a little further and you'll reach it."

Following along the circumference of this huge saltwater lake, he saw a towering mountain standing right next to the lake.

The azure sky and the majestic mountain, from the foot of the mountain the clouds seemed to float around the soaring peak.

The lake below was like a mirror, appearing like a celestial realm.

Yesser was delighted: "This is it."

He climbed up the mountain and pointed to the entrance on both sides of the mountain path, saying:

"Two divine statues need to be carved at the entrance here, one of my father King Laidley, and one of myself."

"The statues should be kneeling on one knee, showing reverence to the divine."

"It's as if welcoming the descent of the divine into the temple we have built for Him."

The temple had not yet been established, but Yesser had already begun to imagine the scene of the divine's descent.

Yesser climbed up the mountain and discussed with several ministers from the Heinze Kingdom on how to construct this place, which would be the greatest miracle created by the Heinze Kingdom, the most precious gift they could offer to the divine.

"In the city bestowed by the divine, there is a statue of my father guarding the divine."

"Here."

"My father and I will jointly guard the divine's temple."

Finally.

They reached the summit, where the air was thin and the wind howled fiercely.

But standing here, it felt like the entire world could be taken in at a glance.

This feeling reminded Yesser of the first time he climbed to the top of the pyramid temple in his youth, but the view here was even more expansive, and the grandeur of this mountain surpassed that of the pyramids.

"It's here, right here."

"Level the entire mountaintop, and then build a temple here, the most magnificent and splendid structure in the world."

"Apart from the priests serving the divine, thousands of people must guard the divine here forever."

The excited Yesser had even thought of a name for this divine temple. Gazing at the floating clouds around him, when this divine temple was completed, it would surely appear to be perched among the clouds when viewed from the foot of the mountain.

"It shall be called."

"The Celestial Temple."

The Celestial Temple that Yesser wanted to build was more like a city than a temple hall.

Yesser was very satisfied with this place, imagining that after the city was built here, he too could gain the divine's favor like his father.

"That's it!"

"It must be built as soon as possible, within five years, it must be completed within five years."

The minister looked up: "Five...five years?"

Yesser asked in a tone that brooked no objection: "Can't do it?"

The minister hurriedly nodded: "No problem, Your Majesty!"

"Five years later, you will definitely see this Celestial Temple."

The king seemed somewhat impatient, and everyone knew that such a huge project would inevitably be time-consuming and laborious.

It's easy to build a city, but the Celestial Temple...

Four years later.

Sky Temple.

Two lifelike giant statues hundreds of meters tall appeared on the cliff, a celestial stairway connected from the bottom of the mountain all the way to the top, linking to the temple floating among the clouds.

The mountaintop was leveled entirely by a mole-like fusion monster, and the sky temple and the city where people serving the gods lived were built on this platform.

The temple was dozens of meters high, extremely luxurious inside.

Aside from the main stone structure quarried locally, many parts used rare stones and gems mined from the sea, which had to be transported from hundreds of miles away or even farther.

A fusion monster was arranged specifically to transport these materials regularly.

Yeser had even found a flawless pure white stone from the seabed, and personally carved the statue of the god Inse, which was hauled to the foot of the mountain today.

This was an architectural and engineering feat akin to a miracle, only possible by mobilizing the power of fusion monsters and priests.

On the steep cliffs above.
