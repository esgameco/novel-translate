This was his first time seeing sunlight, and also the first time he laid eyes on the majestic God-gifted City.

The scorching radiance overhead made him feel dizzy, and the cheers of millions made him excited and flushed.

Yeser followed his father up the stairs that seemed they could never finish climbing, crossing over pyramid-like peaks, heading towards the highest temple.

This path over the stairs made him feel a sacred emotion welling up in his heart.

Since Laidlike brought his eldest son here once before in disappointment, he had never brought anyone up here again.

Even his son, those without the power of myth were unworthy to come before the gods.

In his view.

Those who did not even have the power to see the gods and receive revelations would only defile the will of the gods, polluting the gods' temple.

In front of the temple, two Trifolians, one large and one small, knelt down.

The wise king Laidlike looked at Yeser excitedly and full of anticipation.

"Yeser!"

"Do you see?"

Yeser knelt and looked into the temple, carefully raising his head to let his gaze extend along the floor deep inside.

He saw the shell radiating the light of myth, the supreme instrument for creating life.

Beneath the altar sat a young girl, the fusion monster Shallie surging with terrifying power.

The moment he saw the girl, a huge beast-like mountain appeared before his eyes, with countless hands and hundreds of terrifying eyes staring at him, making him fall into the abyss.

Finally.

He saw the gods.

The gods transcending ancient eons and controlling the laws of time radiated a brilliant light like the stars, the radiance engulfing him, everything in his sight turning dazzling white.

Yeser's gaze was blank, his consciousness became empty.

He and his father cried out the sounds uttered when first seeing the gods.
