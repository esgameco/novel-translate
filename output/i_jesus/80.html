Dream World. 69shux.com Interpretation

The dream fairy Hila and her companions gathered in front of the primordial fish swamp, casting the latest dream magic she had acquired over a decade ago.

Magic art - Ceramic art.

The gods had bestowed upon her the magic art of making ice and snow, the magic art of making oil, metal, and glass, all of which she had already mastered.

The casting materials for ice and snow are water, for oil are fish and insects, for metal are ores, and so on.

But the casting material for ceramics is simply earth.

"La la la la la!"

"The gifts are here!"

Dream fairy Hila tapped the mound of accumulated soil, and a dreamlike starlight began to swirl, and in the blink of an eye, the soil on the ground turned into a series of exquisite white ceramics.

Among them were ceramic statues nearly as tall as a person, delicate but lifelike ceramic figurines, and ceramic tiles with colorful paintings, each one different.

"Ah!"

"How beautiful."

"Wow, Hila is so amazing!"

The small fairies gathered around her couldn't wait to rush forward and select the gifts they liked.

Dream fairy Hila had a smile on her face, feeling very satisfied.

She enjoyed bringing laughter and joy to others, and when the little fairies were happy, she was even happier.

"Hila is so amazing, she can create anything." A small fairy huddled next to Hila, looking at her with admiration.

"It's so beautiful, when can we be like Hila and be able to create whatever we want?" Another small fairy, who had just been born not long ago, joyfully held up her colorful ceramic figurine.

Dream fairy Hila walked into the flower field with the small fairies, and some of them floated up, circling around Hila in the flower field.

"When you're a hundred years old, you can also choose your own dream egg."

"Then you can practice dream magic and create whatever you want."

Hila said to the small fairies.

The small fairies immediately said, "Those big fairies also have dream eggs, but the things they create are so weird."

Other fairies floating like flower petals in the flower field also chirped, "Yes, yes, yes."

"They look so ugly."

"Nothing like how beautiful Hila's creations are."

The small fairies mostly liked Hila, because compared to the other big fairies who always liked to play tricks, she was more like a mature and steady older sister.

The small fairies complained to Hila about how the older and bigger fairies played tricks.

"Last time they created a metal pendant that was a terrible bug."

"And they even created a scary monster statue to scare us."

"Those big fairies are the worst."

At this time, a few heads poked out from the flower field.

The big fairies were angry, they didn't accept the small fairies' criticism: "How are we not good? We're great!"

They glared their eyes: "Yes, yes, yes! It's just that you don't know how to appreciate it."

Some even stuck out their tongues: "Haha haha haha."

The big fairies and small fairies had a huge age gap, but their personalities were quite similar.

Whether small fairies or big fairies, they were all a group of children.

The group of dream fairies started to get noisy again, and the divine flower garden was in chaos.

The big fairies were the second-stage fairies who possessed dream eggs, but their magic arts were not directly bestowed by the gods, and their innate talents were mostly inferior to Hila's.
