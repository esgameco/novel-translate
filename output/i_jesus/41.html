The new mythical life form, though a combination of the powers of life and wisdom, could not be said to transcend the previous two, but rather existed in parallel to them.

Its power was that of dreams and illusions, or one could say the dream realm.

A force born from the powers of life and wisdom, yet distinctly different.

Leadley's power possessed an ability akin to mind-reading, able to manifest thoughts into a supernatural spiritual force. This newborn mythical life could neither read minds nor manifest thoughts into spiritual force, yet it could create dreams and invade the dreams of others.

Shally's power enabled her to create life and gain immortality, able to freely change her form, even altering her size.

It certainly could not achieve immortality, but its lifespan far exceeded that of the Trilians. Its body, like Shally's, was wondrous, possessing the ability to travel between the dream realm and reality.

Yin took out the Sun Cup, and its bud automatically unfurled.

The head within the cup slowly opened its eyes and yawned.

"Ah!"

Then, it saw Yin.

Immediately, it felt uneasy.

It retracted its head back inside, and the unfurled bud tried to close up again.

But Yin reached out his hand, and it could no longer move.

Yin used his wisdom power to input the language of the Kingdom of Hynxei into his mind, then communicated with it.

"Who are you?"

The Sun Cup looked up at Yin with a dazed expression: "Who am I?"

Yin could sense it rapidly familiarizing itself with the knowledge and language he had imparted: "Has anyone given you a name before? Or have you given yourself one?"

The Sun Cup looked around at its surroundings, shrinking back as it gazed at Yin.

"Then...who are you?"

Yin chuckled lightly.

"They all call me God."

"You can call me that too if you like."

The Sun Cup still didn't quite understand: "What is a god?"

Yin had heard this question many times before: "Roughly speaking, it is an existence with immense power, so powerful that mortals can scarcely comprehend it!"

Yin's explanation resonated deeply with this newborn mythical creature.

"Then I want to be called God too."

The Sun Cup's stem swayed, and the fear it had felt moments ago gave way to contemplation, as if taking on a formidable name was of utmost importance, filling it with immense excitement.

"Shall I be called the Flower God?"

"No...I'll be called the Sun God."

"The Illusion God or Dream God would work too."

Its thoughts leapt rapidly, unsettled.

Yin looked at it and said: "God is just a title, not a name."

"When you possess power transcending all else, the name God will be bestowed upon you by others."

Poluo swayed as he headed downwards, his stalk moving no slower than legs, but he still felt it wasn't fast enough.

Wearing just the robe exposing his head, he looked like a child in clothes.

Then,
