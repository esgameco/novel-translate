In the warm sea water nest, the eggs of the Trifolians began to hatch. At first, they did not have a human form, and it would be more accurate to say they resembled worms rather than humans.

However, as they slowly grew up in the ocean, their originally transparent insect shells gradually gained color, becoming opaque and harder.

They survived by preying on insects and ancient creatures near the sea. They had no natural predators.

Years later, they began to take on human form.

At this time, they heard a voice echoing in their minds, the voice of the origin of their wisdom and consciousness, the progenitor traced through their bloodline.

"Come!"

"Come to me."

"Come back!"

Under the powerful summoning force, the Trifolians one by one crawled up from the sea bottom to the shore.

They gathered at the foot of the pyramid stone mill, curiously observing everything around them, gazing up at the temple of the gods.

A being who looked exactly like them stood on the steps of the pyramid, facing the sea.

Leydlig welcomed them at the bottom of the stairs. It was he who had sent out the signal summoning these lifeforms. He stretched out his hand with a smile, truly happy.

Among the crowd, there was a special female Trifolian whose armor was white, different from the others.

This made her appear particularly special, catching Leydlig's attention.

Leydlig was stunned at first, then his heart was stirred with ripples. He did not understand what this was, nor did he know there was an emotion called affection.

In front of the majestic temple, the god Yin was also observing this first batch of Trifolians coming ashore.

"Trifolians?"

"Trifolian insects!"

Raidrike led them to the valley behind the pyramid, carving out one cave after another in the rock face, while using the excavated stones to build a city.

Just as the God Yin had said, he established his own city-state and nation.

These caves connected to the ocean depths, so besides the city in the valley, there was also a city deep underwater that could be accessed through the caves.

They lived on the island, worshipping the deities.

Hunting in the waters, and reproducing offspring in the waters.

This place seemed to become an origin place akin to the mythical Nuwa creating humans, God's Garden of Eden.

This tiny island, insignificant to the Earth, became the beginning of intelligent life, a paradise beyond the human world.

Finally, Raidrike also had a family.

He made the female Trifolian with the white bony carapace his queen, and they naturally gave birth to their first child.

Having a child, Raidrike, the moment he crawled out of the sea onto land, joyfully and eagerly led his child up the pyramid steps.

The small and large figures climbed step-by-step until reaching the temple entrance where they knelt down.

He softly said to his child: "Look!"

"God is watching over you there!"

However, his child looked at the temple interior with a blank expression, seeing nothing but the giant statue and stone altar.

"There's nothing there. Just some stones."

Raidrike felt an icy chill run through his body as he saw the disbelief and endless disappointment in his child's eyes.

His pupils held sadness, loss.

And bewilderment.

He realized his child could not see God.
