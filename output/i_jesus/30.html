Not far from the city of Yesaire, in the sea.

A group of soldiers from the Kingdom of Xin were patrolling on the sea surface, led by two young priests riding strange shrimp creatures. They had just recently been granted the status of priests by the Wise King Yesaire among the younger generation.

Following the orders of the Wise King, the young priests had already run through this sea area over and over again. Finally, one of them could not help but ask:

"Is the God-Gifted City really here?"

"We have counted every single rock in this sea, if such a huge island really existed, at least we should be able to find some traces of it!"

The other priest looked at him with a smile: "Are you questioning the great Wise King? Or are you questioning the power of God?"

The young priest immediately fell silent, not daring to say more.

"I know!"

"I will continue to search diligently, the King's will is the King's will."

Although the other priest had warned the young one, he also felt that their search was merely in vain.

He sighed and retold the story that had been circulating in the Kingdom of Xin.

Everyone knew it, but every time it was told, it felt different.

"The blasphemer Ens killed his own brother Buen and took away the Ruch Giant (Fusion Monster) that God had bestowed upon Buen."

"Ens used the power bestowed by God upon the Trifoliate people, and attempted to assassinate the eldest son of God, his great father the Wise King Redrik."

His voice was melodious, as if chanting a great poem.

"Such a grave sin, appearing in the God-Permitted Paradise, appearing at the foot of the Divine Palace."

"Disappointed and saddened, the Divine brought down divine punishment upon the people of the Kingdom of Xin."

"The descendants of the sinners were forever exiled to the deep abyss of the sea, and the people of Xin could no longer return to their former homeland."

"Oh Divine!"

"When will you forgive our sins and once again shed your brilliance upon the Kingdom of Xin?"

After chanting this widely spread poem in Xin, he shook his head.

"We mortals, how can we set foot in the Divine Palace again before receiving the forgiveness of the Divine."

Although both priests felt that this journey was doomed to be fruitless, they still carried out the will of the Wise King diligently and meticulously.

They split into two routes, going deeper into the sea area.

The young priest led a team of soldiers, parting the waves and swiftly crossing the sea surface, when he caught sight of a streak of gold on the water from afar.

Against the pure azure blue of the vast ocean, that golden brilliance was so conspicuous.

"What is floating on the sea?"

"Let's go take a look."

The group hurried over, and the priest on the strange shrimp creature extended a bone spear to retrieve from the sea surface the golden flower known as the Sun's Chalice.

None of them had ever seen anything like this before, they had never even seen anything golden except for the sun in the sky.

"This is what..."

Waves of golden light floated out from the priest's body. Unconsciously, he released his grip on the Sun's Chalice, allowing it to levitate.

Yet at this moment, everything changed.

His power could leave his body and directly influence the outside world.

The entire squad of soldiers around him, as the golden light emanated from his body, sensed a fragrance that directly acted upon the depths of their minds.

Then.

In shock and horror, as if their consciousness had been taken away, everyone became motionless.

Their bodies froze, slowly sinking beneath the sea.

Fortunately, they could survive underwater, so their lives were not in danger.

But the young priest of the gods was completely unaware, his eyes shining with golden light as his consciousness suddenly appeared on a golden island.

He saw the place where the Sun's Chalice had been.

He stood on the shore, surrounded by a dense golden sea of flowers swaying.

He never imagined that this rather inconspicuous item among the many divine creations would have such a connection to the power of wisdom.

For a moment, he felt as if fate had already been determined.

It seemed as if God's gaze had already seen this moment by looking down the long river of time.

"God!"

"Was everything arranged by You?"

Yesser recalled the words his father had said to him as a child: "Yesser, do not be afraid."

"God has arranged everything."

His sharp gaze turned towards this young priest.

Such a thing could only exist in the divine palace, it was impossible for it to appear here.

"Where did this come from? What did you see?"

Facing Yesser's gaze, Shlodde's body was trembling.

He explained what he had just encountered and everything he had seen.

After listening, Yesser and the elderly Trifoliate next to him had different reactions - one had a joyful and relaxed smile on his face, while the other was so excited that he danced around.

"The land bestowed by God."

Yesser raised the Sun Chalice high, as if holding a supreme sacred object.

This feeling was like when he faced the Crown of Wisdom in the past.

Tears welled up in his eyes, and as he smiled, he let out an impassioned cry:

"This is - God's guidance!"

"God has not abandoned us, He has given us a future again."
