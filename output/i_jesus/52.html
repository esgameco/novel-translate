The ranks of sinners advanced in an orderly and uniform formation, launching incessant attacks on the God-blessed city. All the monsters remained eerily silent, not making the slightest sound or any cries.

But the terror and oppression lurking behind that silence far surpassed any outburst of roaring rage.

On the city walls, the voices of the defending soldiers rose in disarray:

"Kill! Kill them all!"

"Quick, I can't hold this side much longer."

"Throw the stones down, crush these sinners abandoned by God."

"Where is the reserve squad? Where did the reserve squad go?"

"High Priest, High Priest, where are you?"

After a night of fighting, corpses littered both sides of the city walls, and one corner had even collapsed.

The most brutal clashes took place here, the crevices of the collapsed section piled high with corpses.

But the God-descended city managed to hold.

King Ali did not allow the Ruh giants to directly charge into and massacre the God-descended city. He only wanted to stall the Ruh giant in Xing's hands, for in his view, this was his city.

Moreover, he had tens of thousands of sinners at his command, enough to breach the God-descended city without the Ruh giants' assistance in its defense.

At around ten in the morning, both sides stopped fighting of their own accord.

The sinners retreated back into the sea to lick their wounds and regroup.

The God-descended city hurriedly repaired its walls and counted its wounded and dead, preparing for the next battle.

King Ali waited for the sinners to rest and then launch an all-out attack to defeat the defenders and breach the city.

Xing, on the other hand, awaited reinforcements. When the sinners had attacked the previous day, she had sent word through special channels. The fastest Ruh giants could arrive today.

"It's King Ali."

"Someone saw him. The tyrant has returned."

An injured soldier came to report what he had witnessed on the city walls.

Xing had already guessed: "I know."

"You all go and rest! Reinforcements will arrive today."

A few soldiers brought a group of people and held them below the palace.

"Your Majesty."

"These people tried to attack the city gates. We captured them."

The Sky City had initially fallen due to internal traitors opening the gates. Xing could not afford not to be on guard.

She descended the stairs and looked at them.

"Is this how you remain loyal to the king you swore allegiance to?"

But the kneeling nobles said, "You are not our king. You are the king of those vile commoners."

Xing did not get angry. This was not the first time she had heard such words: "Then what was the oath you swore before the gods? Was that sacred vow merely empty words?"

She did not wait for a response, for at this point, arguing and justifying served no purpose.
