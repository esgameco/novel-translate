Yin Shen stood above the vast ocean, treading upon that monstrous giant insect beneath his feet, riding the winds and breaking through the waves as he went.

Here, a day seemed to be shorter, with long days and short nights, and life could only exist within the ocean.

According to Yin Shen's deduction, he was now on Earth nearly 500 million years before his own era.

The Cambrian period.

He had only read about this era in textbooks.

A warm climate, with a brilliant sun during the day, the moon driving the tides, and countless lives thriving and developing vigorously in the sea water.

They were evolving.

All things vying for the ultimate victor's position, contending for the dominance of this planet.

And he himself was an existence that did not belong to this world at all. He could not touch anything, nor could he interfere with the existence of the universe.

Except for those two life forms that sensed his presence.

Only they could observe Yin Shen's existence, and the only objects Yin Shen could touch were the two of them.

Perhaps, to this universe, he did not actually exist.

Just like a ghost lingering in the crevices of space-time.

The trilobite and the worm, by glimpsing Yin Shen's true form transcending the universe and time, also gained power from him - power derived from what Yin Shen had previously called the soul.

The two primordial life forms of the ancient Earth gained different powers. One gained wisdom from Yin Shen, while the other was granted the ability to fuse by his grace.

The trilobite gained Yin Shen's wisdom. Within its hard, armor-like shell, an organ resembling the human brain grew.

It knew some human vocabulary, glimpsing scenes from Yin Shen's memories at the moment of its birth, possessing advanced creative thinking abilities akin to humans.

As for the worm, it gained a more peculiar and mysterious power. Its blood fused with Yin Shen's power, undergoing changes under the radiation of his true form.

It could fuse with other life forms, stripping away their organs and abilities, freely altering its own form.

But regrettably, the worm still acted on instinct, just like before.

The one that gained wisdom was called the Trilobite Man by Yin Shen.

And that worm was named the Fusion Monster by him.

Beneath his feet, the being called the Fusion Monster was rapidly shuttling across the sea surface, a single tentacle swinging rapidly from under its shell to propel it forward.

Yin Shen stood atop its shell, gazing into the distance.

In the distance above the sea surface, something different finally appeared.

Objects.

Something protruded above the horizontal surface, casting long shadows on the sea under the last rays of the setting sun.

"Swish swish." The fusion monster swam rapidly, its dense tentacles stirring the seawater.

Shen Yin saw a solitary island, on which there was rare greenery.

The island was pitted with hollows and patches of marsh, covered in lichens and mosses.

But compared to other lands, this place was already full of the breath of life.

"Not bad here," said Shen Yin, deciding to stay and end his aimless long journey.

The next day.

Shen Yin saw the fusion monster use its dexterous tentacles to quarry huge rocks from the seabed and drag them onto the island.

Its tentacles could become sharp tools like chisels or blunt ones like hammers, striking and splitting these massive rocks.

At first, Shen Yin thought this bug just had a hobby of dragging stones and paid no attention. But when he saw it stack the huge rocks into a structure, he realized it was a building.

Looking closely, this thing was actually similar to the pyramids Shen Yin had seen on TV before, but this one was even more magnificent.

Rows of massive stones tens of meters high and wide were stacked up, making one feel this could absolutely not be a human construction, but a divine miracle.

However, as the rocks reached the highest point, it took a different turn - the fusion monster started stacking and carving an ancient, solemn temple, carefully grinding, striking, and sculpting the details.

It still appeared rough and ancient, but the outline of the temple had emerged.

This temple was clearly not built for the monster itself - it was too small for its huge size.

Shen Yin then realized the fusion monster was constructing a massive palace for him on the isolated island.

Initially, he thought the fusion monster was trying to curry favor with him, but realized that couldn't be right - the chaotic and ignorant fusion monster lacked such intelligence and didn't know about the existence of pyramids.

It must have been ordered to do so by the Trifoliate.

The Trifoliate groveled anxiously before Shen Yin, who gazed calmly at the waves, his emotions serene.

Even when he still had a human body, he was a rather indifferent person. Now, having lost his body and undergone the strangest, most bizarre experiences in the world, he had become even more calm.

Yet the Trifoliate was afraid, not knowing whether his self-appointed god liked this or not.

He explained apprehensively while constantly kowtowing.

Inside, this was the most direct way to make requests and seek forgiveness from the deity.

"God!"

"Palace...temple..."

Yinshen could sense his meaning.

He said the god should reside in a palace, receiving offerings from all life, so he had the fusion monster build this structure.

Yinshen was not joyful, or rather, he did not care much.

After all, he had no physical form, could not feel warmth or cold, and a sheltering structure meant nothing to him. So-called faith and prayers also seemed ridiculous to him, since the ones worshipping and believing in him were just two insects.

If he had to say anything, he could only praise it as interesting!

"Not bad!"

"Very beautiful."

Just those few short words made the Three-Leaf person jump for joy, ecstatic.

The palace was finally complete.

The Three-Leaf person waited for Yinshen on the pyramid. The fusion monster carrying Yinshen climbed towards the top of the pyramid, then stopped, pressing its head against the temple entrance.

At the pyramid's apex, the ancient, solemn hall opened its large doors and windows, with no furnishings inside.

The only highlights were its massive, majestic scale.

Inside was also a statue of Yinshen, hand-carved by the Three-Leaf person as a gift to the deity.

It seemed the Three-Leaf person had inherited the dexterous hands of humans, and possessed strength far exceeding humans.

This deity statue was carved from a single unknown white stone block, more like a relief sculpture than a statue, placed facing the round window towards the sun.

On it was a figure emanating endless radiance, appearing to be draped in white robes, with only the outline visible and features indistinguishable.

This was how they observed Yinshen's appearance.

The Three-Leaf person prostrated on the ground, still addressing Yinshen as god.

This was a general term.

Yinshen told him his name: "Yinshen."

Three-Leaf person: "Yincai!"

The man corrected him again: "Yinshen."

Three-Leaf person: "Yincai!"

He could pronounce the single word, but not when combined.

The Three-Leaf person's vocal organs were still different from humans, producing dry, shrill syllables that sounded grating.

Yinshen corrected him again: "Yinshen!"

The Three-Leaf person lifted his head, ah-ed for a while, and finally called out:

"Yincai...shen!"

"Yincaishen!"

Yinshen let it go, allowing him to call out Yincaishen, Yincaishen, fervently shouting around the statue and Yinshen himself.
