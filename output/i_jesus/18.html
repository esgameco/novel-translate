Inside the temple, Sally obediently stood in front of the huge mythical spiral shell embedded in the wall, her eyes looking through the luminous shell at the strange creatures inside. She was holding a flower pot with a golden cup-shaped flower.

She seemed to really like the scent of this flower.

The God Yin instructed her to take care of this flower, so she always held the flower pot, smelling the fragrance of this bloom.

Suddenly.

The flower in the pot suddenly moved, the golden cup body split open turning into a bloody maw, gnashing towards Sally with a chilling hiss.

"Hiss!"

This was the true form of this flower, a bizarre life between animal and plant.

But faced with those fangs closing in on her, Sally remained motionless, as if she felt no danger at all. Only her originally still green eyes moved like glass balls, falling on the flower pot.

Darkness was spreading, a terrifying shadow appeared behind Sally, the aura of a mythical life at the top of the food chain enveloping this place.

The ferocious drooling flower immediately cowered, not daring to move again.

Facts proved.

On this planet, she was the most terrifying freak queen.

This was the Sun Cup Flower created by God Yin, randomly fused and modified based on a creature called the Ancient Cup from the bottom of the sea.

He planned to create more plants, then plant them on this island to see which could survive, adding some life colors to the monotonous desolation here.

But they could probably only grow on this island of the God-Gifted City, unable to withstand the radiation outside. In this era, only the ocean was a paradise for life.

The miracle of the Trifoliate People belonged only to them, not other lifeforms.

And in the depths of the temple.

Redrick knelt under the divine altar, endlessly telling the deity about his sons and daughters who had returned.

The gifts they presented to him, how much he liked them, even if he played it down in front of the children.

Their every achievement out there, Redrick could recount them all. He also knew how the citizens of the God-Gifted City spread the tales of their adventures.

The children's accomplishments made Redrick happier than his own great achievements.
