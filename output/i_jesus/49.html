The newly crowned Queen Star was preparing to abolish the slave system, to release those miserable people from the fate imposed upon them in the name and power of God by King Yeser.

Or one could say, the curse passed down generation after generation.

She had witnessed with her own eyes the wretched state of the slaves, and was horrified by the terror that could not be changed from one generation to the next.

She was ready to amend the laws. As long as it was stipulated in the laws, even if those slaves were forever branded with the mark of punishment by the Wisdom Kings, it would be forbidden for nobles and merchants to buy and sell them as commodities, to slaughter them like fish and meat.

Inside the palace, several ministers and a group of nobles were infuriated.

"This is tradition, this is the law."

"It is recorded in the legal codes written by the Wisdom Kings that slaves are guilty people who should be punished."

Star was not intimidated by the actions of the ministers and nobles. She was not a strong-willed person, but when it came to the right thing, she had an indescribable stubbornness.

"Then amend the legal codes."

The ministers firmly disagreed: "Those are the legal codes set by the Wisdom Kings, the laws laid down by King Yeser."

"Even if you are the current king, you should uphold the will of the former kings."

Star sat motionless on the throne, but her gaze did not waver: "What if King Yeser was wrong?"

The minister said, "King Yeser could not be wrong."

The difficulty far exceeded Star's expectations.

It was obvious that almost all the powerful figures and nobles vehemently opposed Star's actions.

Without slaves, who would tend to their fisheries? How would their bone workshops operate?

Who would be the laborers for their merchant caravans? Who would guarantee and serve their luxurious lives?

The situation was at a stalemate.

But even if the royal power had declined, it was not something ordinary nobles could resist. The ministers and powerful figures under Star were more timid and fearful.

Someone came out from the crowd and knelt down on the steps before the throne.

"Your Majesty!"

"I have heard your decree, but amending the legal codes is not a simple matter. It requires convening scholars who compiled the codes for research, which will take time."
