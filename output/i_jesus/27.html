Yin Shen stood at a high place watching the crowds leave the God-Gifted City, with one fusion monster after another clearing the path ahead.

The Trifolians returned to the sea once more. A few holders of wisdom and power controlled the fusion monsters, driving away the dense schools of fish that followed the setting sun, leaving this paradise that belonged to him, Leadri, and Shari.

The entire world fell silent, the once prosperous city becoming deserted and empty.

Yin Shen turned around and saw Leadri kneeling before his own statue.

"Leadri."

"It's fine to leave this place, we don't need so many people accompanying us."

"The sea and land are the destiny of civilization, this is just an island."

He wanted to pat Leadri's shoulder like he used to, but his hand passed right through Leadri's body.

This made Yin Shen stunned for a moment before realizing the reason.

Leadri had given all his mythical blood to his descendants and the Crown of Wisdom, this body was just an ordinary shell, no longer possessing the power Yin Shen had bestowed upon Leadri, having also lost the ability to anchor.

Yin Shen looked at his luminous hand and was suddenly overwhelmed by intense, nameless rage.

"Am I dead or alive?"

Yin Shen wanted to smash everything in front of him, but lowered his raised hand, as anger and impatience were futile.

The only thing he could rely on was endless time and life.

He could change everything, accomplish anything, needing only to wait.

He went from fearing loneliness to loathing it.

Now, he had grown accustomed to loneliness.

A god.

Without companions.

He sat on the steps of the temple, gazing at the tranquil God-Gifted Land.

Watching the sun's light and shadows creep across the buildings of the God-Gifted City from the shore, seeing the moon traverse from one side of the night sea to the other.

After the sun rose and set a few cycles, he finally stood up.

"Shari!"

The girl turned her head towards him, her big eyes staring at him.

She trotted over to Yin Shen, craning her neck and making sounds.

"Goo roo roo!"

Yin Shen headed down the steps: "Let's go for a walk."

The two walked down from the temple, Shari cradling a flower pot.

Yin Shen stood for a while before the statue Yeser built for Leadri, then wandered around the silent God-Gifted City, also visiting the marshes that once farmed the ancestral fish.

Finally, he stopped at the seashore.
