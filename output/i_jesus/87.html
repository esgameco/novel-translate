The entire City of the Divine Servants, including the holy mountain above and below, has recently entered a period of ritual bathing and purification. Thousands upon thousands of people kneel and pray facing the Sky Temple, their voices chanting the Lederik Oath merging together like waves echoing across the heavens.

Inside the Sky Temple, Queen Xinglu is kneeling nervously before the divine spirits, preparing for the ritual to open the gates to the Realm of the Gods.

Young Stan Tito stands behind her, somewhat reserved, as he notices the queen's tension and pressure.

"Your Majesty!"

"Are you also afraid?"

Hearing Stan's words, Queen Xinglu suddenly relaxes a great deal.

"Of course! How can one not have a sense of reverence when facing the great divine spirits?"

One is a descendant of the royal bloodline, the offspring of King Lederik.

The other is a saint who brought back divine artifacts from the Realm of the Gods after the Age of the Divine had ended, the heir of the great poet Tito.

In this moment, only they are able to stand before the statue of the god Yesir, carved by the king's own hands.

Even the priests of the Sky Temple can only stand in the outer corridors at this time.

After completing all the preparations, Queen Xinglu takes up the bone tablet and chants the verses left behind by the great poet.

At the end, she recites the passage that connects to the dream world, praying for a response from the fairy guardian of the dream realm.

"Guardian of the Garden of the Gods, divine messenger of the dream world.

Oh fairy of hope, I humbly beseech you to answer my prayer."

"Hira!"

"I pray that you open the gates to the Realm of the Divine, to welcome the wanderers home."

As the queen utters the fairy's true name, the sky above the holy mountain immediately begins to change.

Colorful, dreamlike starlight gathers and swirls amidst the clouds, as if a rift has opened to another world, allowing it to flow out.

Seeing this, the entire City of the Divine Servants erupts in excitement.

"It's coming!" the devout followers cry out, one after another.

"It's arriving!" They pound the floor with their foreheads, as if the harder they strike, the more fervent their faith.

"The gates to the Realm of the Gods are about to open," the priests in front of the temple shout, nearly frenzied.

They had never before witnessed the arrival of a miracle in the City of the Divine, but this time, they are all participants.

Everyone looks up, waiting for the final moment.

However, only the dreamlike starlight appears, and the gates to the divine realm do not open.

Eventually, the starlight fades and disappears.

It disappears?

The excitement, clamor, and restlessness of the City of the Divine Servants gives way to utter silence.

The people stare at the sky, expressions blank.

The priests, their hands raised, forget to lower them.

Queen Xinglu, watching the dissipating starlight and the clearing sky, is unable to accept what has happened.

"Why?" she asks, distraught, even panicked. "Why did this happen?

Is it that the divine spirits do not accept my faith?

Or have I done something wrong?"

The queen begins to retreat, nearly falling to the ground.

Stan Tito supports her, and the temple priests gather around.

"Perhaps, Your Majesty, it is because a key element was missing," one of the priests speculates.

"We only obtained one page of the great poet's final chapter, while the saint Tito left behind an entire volume of poems.

Perhaps only when the complete poems of the great poet are present will we have the proof and talisman to open the gates to the divine."

Queen Xinglu asks, "Is that truly the case?"

The priest replies, "I believe so."
