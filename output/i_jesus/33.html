"It's you, King Yesser."

"You are unworthy to wear the crown of the Wise King, you are unworthy to possess the legacy of King Laidley..."

The words were not finished before they came to an abrupt halt.

For the first time, Yesser widely employed the power of the Wise King, stretching out his hand towards the thousands of Trifolians kneeling before him. Light and shadows flowed out from between his fingers, and in an instant, the eyes of all the kneeling Trifolians turned hollow.

Their self-awareness was twisted from the root, their memories completely modified, turning them into obedient slaves.

At the same time, marks of the King's punishment appeared on the bone helmets on their foreheads. This was the mark of a slave.

This mark would be passed down through their bloodlines, their descendants forever unable to escape the fate of slavery.

They could never erase the imprint left by the power of the Wise King through their own efforts.

When Laidley first widely used the power of the Wise King, it was in hopes that his descendants would inherit his covenant and vows with the gods.

But Yesser did so to punish his defiant subjects.

A noble of the Heinze Kingdom behind him nervously stepped forward and knelt before Yesser, asking:

"Your Majesty!"

"This..."

"Should the construction of the Sky Temple continue?"

The rampaging fusion monster had destroyed large swaths of the construction, ruining the divine stones that took years to find and the deity statues personally carved by the King himself. With so many experienced craftsmen having their memories and wills twisted into slaves, it would surely be impossible to complete on schedule. Even with four more years, it would be difficult - some of the rare treasures lost could never be found again, even at the bottom of the sea.

Those were decades of the Heinze Kingdom's treasuries - once gone, they were gone forever.

Yesser didn't even look at him: "Of course it must continue!"

"And it must be built better, more perfect."

"This time, no mistakes will be allowed."

The noble's body trembled as he asked in trepidation:

"Your Majesty!"

"When will it be completed?"

The previously impatient king suddenly became less overbearing.

"Within ten years."

He paused: "Besides these slaves, there are also craftsmen already conscripted and prepared to be brought over. No further conscription is needed for now."

After speaking, he boarded the fusion monster and turned to leave, with hundreds of guards following closely behind.

After venting his anger, Yesser suddenly felt a weariness all over his body.

The distant sea.

A huge group of terrifying beings emerging from the deep sea trenches pierced through the surface, entering this sea territory belonging to the Trifolians, snatching at the scattered ancestral fish shoals like hungry wolves.

They had insect-like shells, their entire elongated lower bodies encased in soft shells, resembling shrimp tails.

Their upper bodies were similar to Trifolians, except Trifolians had bone armor while theirs were a kind of soft shell armor.

Their two arms no longer had dexterous hands, instead becoming two massive cones.

On the surface of the sea.

The citizens of the Hope Kingdom responsible for herding the fish shoals finally discovered these beings, seeing the dense mass of monsters charging out.

"Th-th-this..."

A shrill voice erupted: "What are these things?"

An old man immediately recognized what they were: "Sinners!"

"Sinners punished by the divine beings, they've burst out from the abyss of evil."

They didn't get far before being caught up by these monsters.

Their shrimp tail-like lower bodies were more suited for darting and roaming in the water.

Their cone-like arms pierced through their chests, instantly killing them on the spot.

These monsters were indiscriminate in their feeding, immediately devouring these Trifolians.

The scene was bloody and horrific.

These monsters were the descendants of the former Ence and Brun, having lost the higher intelligence of Trifolians, only retaining beast-like cooperation and instincts.

They had no language, unable to speak.

But the antennae on their heads could touch and communicate thoughts. Thousands upon thousands of sinners gathered together, like a terrifying monster army.

The originally nearly one hundred sinners, in just decades breeding madly in the abyss of evil, their numbers became nearly ten thousand.
