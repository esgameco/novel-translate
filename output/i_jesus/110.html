Externally and internally, he had become more mature, more steady, and more self-assured.

He had once been hailed as the "Lucky Son" in the City of the Divine Servants, but that had left him feeling ethereal and weightless, floating in midair.

Now, he had lost that former status, yet people felt he had gained even more.

He sat quietly by the seaside, like a towering boulder standing at the edge of the vast ocean, blocking the surging waves.

As if allowing the mighty waves to crash against him, he remained unwavering.

Heniel's voice came through the messenger's shoulder-mounted message bug, reaching the mind of Stanton Tito.

"The war is about to end."

"The endless infighting, slaughter, and attrition between the royal bloodlines over the centuries is finally going to be put to a stop, and the glory of Hinsai will be restored."

"All you have to do is come back."

"You are still the Lucky Son, and I still have the position of divine servant waiting for you in the Sky Temple."

He had come to see this old friend, but also to flaunt and showcase his own accomplishments.

Stanton Tito did not turn back, he was solely focused on his stone carving work.

"I know you did not kill the Queen, but you witnessed her death and did nothing."

"You have no loyalty, your heart is filled only with ambition and greed."

Heniel was unconcerned: "I've heard that kind of talk many times before, but what of ambition and greed? What of schemes and deceptions?"

"I cannot wash these things away, nor do I intend to."

"Let people say what they will, I am who I am, I am King Heniel."

"As long as I can surpass other kings and achieve things never before seen, no one will care about these things."

"Come back, Stanton Tito. I am the king, you are the emissary of the gods, together we will forge a new era."

Stanton Tito remained immersed in his work, paying no heed to Heniel.

After a long while, he finally paused his carving.

"Why must you choose me?"

"Isn't another member of the Tito family always vying to become the heir to the will of the Saint?"

Heniel's voice suddenly changed, becoming mocking and disdainful.

"Because! They are not worthy."

Stanton Tito turned back: "Am I then worthy?"

Heniel nodded: "Of course, you are worthy of the title of Saint's Heir, for you are the true inheritor of the great poet's will and power."

Stanton Tito did not respond, but suddenly asked Heniel a seemingly unrelated question.

"Are you willing to renounce the power of the Colossus?"

Heniel was taken aback, feeling this question was strangely familiar.

"This question..."

"The emissary of the gods had once asked me the same."

Stanton Tito turned his gaze back: "And how did you answer?"

Heniel: "I refused."

Stanton Tito looked at Heniel with puzzlement: "Do you know?"

"Your refusal may have forfeited the future of the Trifoliate people."

Heniel told Stanton Tito: "I cannot see the future, I only know to seize the present."

Stanton Tito returned his focus to his carving.

"You may be able to reunite Hinsai, you may be able to build a great legacy, becoming a king as great as the former King Yessir."

"But in you, I see no hope or light."

Heniel was even more perplexed: "Is not being comparable to King Yessir enough?"

Stanton Tito let out a soft laugh: "For a king, it may be enough."

"But for the Trifoliate people, for the descendants of Lerdellick, for our civilization..."

"It is not enough! Far from enough."

Stanton Tito extended his hand, gazing out at the vast sea.

"The world is moving forward, the river of time flows endlessly."

"Why are we the only ones standing still?"

"The civilization founded by King Lerdellick, has it already become the dying embers of sunset in the time of his son, King Yessir?"

"I refuse to believe it."

"This is not what King Lerdellick wanted to see."

Heniel said no more, feeling that the Stanton Tito before him had changed completely.

He had once greatly admired Stanton Tito, thinking they could become friends, but now he felt the other was different, even seeing the shadow of the great poet in him.

That was...

The existence known as the Saint.

The divine power receded, Heniel's voice gradually faded away.

"You have truly changed, no longer a mere puppet on strings, nor the Lucky Son."

"A person like you will not remain here forever as a mere craftsman."
