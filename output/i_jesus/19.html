The queen was ill, gravely ill. On the third floor of the Palace of Wisdom, the queen sat in the stone room by the window, looking out through the window. She had personally witnessed this place transform from a desolate wilderness into a magnificent city. She had also personally helped the Wise King build the first house of the God-blessed city.

The kindled flames lit the path towards civilization.

She had once watched the sun rise and shine upon God's pyramids and the barren valley, and now she watched the setting sun's last rays fall upon the towering buildings of different heights in the God-blessed city.

The sun of civilization had just risen, yet she was about to exit the stage.

Laidlike came up behind her stone chair and embraced her from behind.

"I've told the children."

"I want them to cross the sea to seek the land where God descended, the land of origins, to fulfill my final dream."

A proud smile of a life's accomplishments appeared on the face of the Wise King.

"The one who finds the land of origins."

"I will give him the throne, and personally crown him king."

Only then did the queen turn her attention back from the scenery outside the window to look at the also aged Laidlike.

"Isn't this too rushed?"

"Some things can be left for the children to do, there's no need to accomplish everything in our generation."

"Moving too quickly will put too much pressure on the children, and in the end, the result may not be what we imagined."

The queen stroked Laidlike's face, her eyes full of tenderness.

"We've grown old."

"We should also stop and rest, shouldn't we, my king?"

Laidlike shook his head, though aged, the ambition could still be seen in his eyes.

He had established the God-blessed city, founded the Hyksos Kingdom, ignited the seeds of civilization and let them blaze brilliantly.

Just one more step.

Just one more step.

He believed he could do it, just as he had accomplished those great achievements before, because he was the Wise King, God's favored son.

"I cannot wait any longer."

"We have conquered the ocean, we should set foot on land again."

"We are God's eldest sons, destined to rule the ocean and land."

"At least before I die, I want to see us control the land and sea, so that I can die without regrets before God."

"I will tell God."

"I have done it."

"I have fulfilled his expectations of me, and will also present my vow before him."

The queen looked at him doubtfully: "A vow?"

Laidlike smiled but did not speak.

Laidlike thought many would come looking for him, but he waited until nightfall and no one came.

After dark, of the seven children, only Yesser came.
