The God-gifted Land.

A sea of flowers with golden sun cups blanketed the ruined city, and Sali was the only being able to move freely on this vast island - the entire island was her garden.

Normally she would sit on the steps before the divine temple, imitating the divine in watching the sunset and moon, or gaze up at the statue of the divine deep inside the temple.

She would look for an entire day.

Sometimes she would also stroll through the sea of flowers amidst the ruined city, brushing aside the sun cup floral crowns and inhaling the fragrance of the flowers.

These sun cups had cracked apart the once neatly paved stone paths, and Sali waded through the towering sea of flowers taller than herself in the ruined city, arriving at the seaside.

She came to the place where the divine had once instructed her to plant the first sun cup, picking up the discarded stone planter she had thrown away before.
