The chaos on the Taishan Road was within Gu Qingfeng's expectations.

Resources, be they abundant or scarce, were always coveted by various forces. The Taishan Road was among the top ten in Guangyang Prefecture, so it was natural that these powers would not miss the opportunity.

When the Qingyang Sect was still guarding the Taishan Road, other forces, though envious, did not dare to make a move easily. It was simply too risky to provoke a powerful sect.

As for this infighting among the sects and families, Gu Qingfeng had no intention of interfering. Occupying the entire White Stone Sect and the abundant resources it provided were already sufficient for the development of the Gu family manor.

In the short term, even if the Gu family manor were to seize control of the Taishan Road, it would not significantly enhance their strength. On the contrary, such an action would make the Gu family manor a target for all the other powers.

Gu Qingfeng understood that there was a fundamental difference between the martial prowess of the Gu family and the occult arts of the Zhuo and Jianqin families.

Therefore, when Zhuo Feng came knocking, Gu Qingfeng readily ceded the Taishan Road in exchange for a certain price. This arrangement was entirely beneficial to the Gu family manor, with no downsides.

Of course, if these forces dared to interfere with the White Stone Sect, Gu Qingfeng would not hesitate to act.
