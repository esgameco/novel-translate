Chapter 33

In Meng Tian's residence.

Meng Tian was preparing for the expedition to the north.

The emperor attached great importance to this war, and so did he.

The northern barbarians had big plans for this northward invasion.

He must lead the army to stop the northern barbarians and let them know that the borders of the Great Qin are not where they should come!

The soldiers of the Great Qin are not to be trifled with!

Therefore, Meng Tian personally took care of every detail to ensure there were no mistakes.

As Meng Tian was packing up the border defense maps and some materials, the housekeeper hurried in.

He reported to Meng Tian, "General, the emperor has sent a letter."

With that said, the housekeeper handed the letter to Meng Tian.

Taking the letter, it indeed had the emperor's seal on it.

Meng Tian was surprised.

Previously, when he discussed the war against the northern barbarians with the emperor, the emperor had already given all the necessary instructions.

Why did the emperor send another letter now?

Could it be that the emperor had some new ideas?

Meng Tian opened the letter and read the contents carefully.

However, after reading it twice, Meng Tian became even more puzzled.

In the letter, the emperor reminded him to be careful on this northward expedition.

He must always carry things like bows, arrows, and oil.

And if he encounters mysterious enemies, he should not act rashly, let alone charge head-on.

Instead, he should make full use of the cavalry's mobility to gradually wear down the enemy...

These very specific suggestions left Meng Tian somewhat at a loss.

What did the emperor mean by this?

He hadn't even gone north yet and didn't know the enemy's situation.

How could the emperor seem to know what kind of enemy he would encounter?

It was as if the emperor even knew what tactics the enemy would employ.

Could it be that the emperor had planted spies among the northern barbarians?

Or did he have informants reporting to him?

Why had the emperor never mentioned this before?

No matter how much Meng Tian thought about it, he couldn't understand what was going on.

However, Meng Tian's advantage was that he was loyal and obedient enough.

Even if he had many questions in his mind, he would still do as the emperor instructed.

He would not be obstinate or act on his own.

Ying Zheng knew this very well, which was why he wrote the letter to specifically instruct him.

Sure enough, Meng Tian kept these instructions firmly in mind.

He planned to use these methods to deal with the situations the emperor mentioned if he encountered them on the battlefield.

On the other side, outside the Shi Ya Prison, the members of the Flowing Sand group had already gathered.

This time they were going to break into the famous Shi Ya Prison, so they dared not take it lightly. Basically, everyone who could come had come.

Wei Zhuang looked at the intelligence about Shi Ya Prison obtained from Yan Ri, frowning.

It was no other reason than that Shi Ya Prison was too difficult to break into.

The entrance was hidden beneath the seawater and could only be discovered during low tide. When the tide rose, people inside would not be able to come out.

The Devouring Fang Prison itself was a huge mechanical fortress, with all parts capable of moving.

The techniques of Qimen (mysterious gate) were ever-changing. The Qimen changes adopted by the Devouring Fang Prison were divided into two types: the Pai Gong method and the Fei Gong method.

The Pai Gong method had 262,144 variations, while the Fei Gong method had 531,441 variations.

The combinations of the two resulted in even more variations, a staggering number that could not be solved by human power alone.

Without mastering the Qimen calculation method, sending in more people would only mean sending them to their deaths.

No wonder Ying Zheng chose this place as a prison.

If one were to name the most impregnable places in the world, the Devouring Fang Prison would definitely be on the list.

Chi Lian had no idea what difficulties they would encounter. She asked, "Zhuang, when do we take action?"

After careful consideration, Wei Zhuang said to everyone, "For this operation, I will go in alone, and you will provide support from the outside."

Chi Lian, Bai Feng, Wu Shuang Gui, Cang Lang Wang, Yin Fu, and others suddenly raised their heads to look at Wei Zhuang.

"You're going in alone?!"

"How can that be?!"

"It's too dangerous! We can't let you take the risk alone!"

Chi Lian disagreed even more, "No, it's too risky for you to go alone. Take me with you, we'll go together and can look out for each other."

Wei Zhuang shook his head and explained to everyone, "The terror of the Devouring Fang Prison is not as simple as you imagine. The Qimen techniques inside are ever-changing, and having more people doesn't mean an advantage."

"The hundreds of thousands of variations inside require initial layout and calculation of patterns to solve."

"You don't understand the Qimen variations, so going in will only be a hindrance."

Chi Lian was a bit unconvinced.

Even if they didn't understand those Qimen calculations, having more people would always provide some support and wouldn't be a bad thing, so how could it be a hindrance?

But she didn't know how to persuade Wei Zhuang.

Once Wei Zhuang made a decision, no one could change his mind.

Bai Feng didn't care about Wei Zhuang's arrangement.

Not going in was just as well, as her pet White Phoenix wouldn't be able to fully utilize its abilities in the enclosed space of the Devouring Fang Prison.

If it went in, it would only cause trouble.

Seeing that the others weren't saying anything and seemed to have accepted Wei Zhuang's arrangement, Chi Lian couldn't help but feel anxious.

She still wanted to say something and grabbed Wei Zhuang's sleeve.

Wei Zhuang brushed off her hand, his voice filled with unprecedented determination, "This is an order."

With that, he turned and left alone.

Soon after, his presence disappeared.
