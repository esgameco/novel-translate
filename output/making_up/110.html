Chapter 110: Awakening of the Sword Soul, Tyrannical Sword Intent

Meng Chong nurtured the treasured sword with his qi, blood, and spiritual essence every day. The sword resonated with his heart, and his intent merged with it. When faced with a life-and-death crisis, the time had finally come to draw the sword.

The sword emerged, and the sword soul awakened. The sword intent shook the four corners of the world.

The sword light seemed to tear the heavens and earth apart, tyrannical and unparalleled, unstoppable!

On the city tower of the Wu capital, the onlooking Wu Emperor and his people could only see that one sword light. Even from afar, it exuded an aura of dominating the world.

Their hearts were gripped as if by a hand, their breathing stagnant, eyes wide open, and expressions filled with shock.

The unparalleled sword light, the tyrannical sword intent, shattered the pressure exerted by the Blood Spirit Sect Patriarch and pierced his consciousness.

"How is this possible? What kind of sword technique is this?" the Blood Spirit Sect Patriarch exclaimed in horror.

He tried to block Meng Chong's strike with his own sword.

He wanted to flee and evade, but it was all in vain!

With a single slash, the world seemed to lose its color. The unparalleled sword intent instantly struck the Blood Spirit Sect Patriarch.

Poof!

The serrated sword was blown away.

The body of the senior from the Wu Clan was cleaved in two.

The blood spirit worm was also severed into two halves, each part residing in the two halves of the corpse.

"Impossible, impossible! What power is this? How can he kill me? He's not even a Sect Master, yet I am a Cultivated Celestial..." the Blood Spirit Sect Patriarch exclaimed in horror.

His consciousness was filled with unwillingness and disbelief.

He had thought that this young man would be the cornerstone of his resurgence, that the heavens would not abandon him, the Blood Spirit Sect Patriarch.

Yet, he was going to die here completely!

He was unwilling, utterly unwilling!

In the past, when many powerful experts had surrounded and attacked him, he had still managed to cling to life and wait for the opportunity to make a comeback.

But in the end, he was going to die completely in this remote wilderness?

Thump! Thump! Thump!

Meng Chong's heart was pounding violently, and there was a wound on his chest. The bleeding had already slowed, but it would still take a few days of rest to fully heal.

This battle had almost cost him his life!

At the last moment, the sword nurturing succeeded, and he drew the sword to strike!

The power of the sword he had nurtured for so long was terrifying. Even a Sect Master could be slain with a single stroke!

What delighted him even more was that in this critical moment, not only did he draw the sword, but he also awakened the sword soul and comprehended the sword intent!

Tyrannical and unparalleled sword intent!

Huff! Huff! Huff!

Meng Chong panted, looking at the treasured sword in his hand. At this moment, he felt the sword's uniqueness, as if it could resonate with him.

It seemed that he had imbued this sword with a soul.

Even the serrated sword left behind by the Blood Spirit Sect Patriarch seemed to be responding to him, drawn by his spiritual awareness.

Sword soul!

"So this is the awakening of the sword soul?" Meng Chong felt that with the sword soul awakened, there was no sword in the world that would not respond to him.

Moreover, he had also comprehended the sword intent.

"I have comprehended the sword intent, before even breaking through to the Xiantian Realm. This is a significant gain."

Meng Chong was greatly elated in his heart.

Although this battle was extremely perilous, and he had almost died, the harvests were also immense.

"My senior brother was right. A warrior must understand combat. Only by fighting can one more easily grasp the true essence of the martial dao."

Meng Chong stepped forward, approaching the severed corpse.

His master had taught him that for any enemy, simply cutting off their head was not enough. One must grind the bones and scatter the ashes to ensure their complete destruction and prevent any chance of survival.

"This slash of mine dominates the world. My sword dao has now entered the gate, and my sword intent shall be called the Tyrannical Sword Intent, perfectly fitting for me."

Meng Chong thought to himself.

Gripping the sword, he stepped forward, his body enveloped in golden light, the image of a golden bell covering him, and the sword intent swirling around him. He did not dare to be careless.

This senior from the Wu Clan was not quite right.

Without even reaching him, the sword had already struck, instantly cleaving one half of the corpse.

"What is this?"

Suddenly, Meng Chong noticed a few silver-white small grasses scattered around the corpse, seemingly pulsing with spiritual energy.

"Could this be a spiritual herb?"

Meng Chong's heart rejoiced. He had heard his junior sister talk about spiritual herbs often, and the medicinal pills refined from them were said to have astounding effects.

With a wave of his hand, he moved the silver-white grasses aside, cautiously avoiding touching them.

He then turned his gaze to the corpse.

His eyes gleamed with ruthlessness. He had almost died!

Boom!

He unleashed a powerful punch, enveloping the corpse in his blazing qi and blood.

The sword intent also shrouded it.

Poof!

The corpse was crushed to ashes, incinerated by the scorching aura.

Under the coverage of the sword intent, Meng Chong suddenly detected a faint pressure, assaulting his sword intent.

He saw the severed worm!

It was this mysterious thing that was emanating the pressure!

However, the pressure was weak and had been completely suppressed by his sword intent.

"Die!"

Meng Chong did not dare to be careless. He swiftly brought down his sword.

The worm had to be completely destroyed!

"Young man, I am..." the Blood Spirit Sect Patriarch exclaimed in fury and horror, even though his consciousness was fading, for he had not yet died completely.

Perhaps there was still a chance for him to survive, if someone came to collect the corpse in the Wu capital, or if someone came to inspect the battlefield and he could seize their body.

Although he could not devour the bait and synchronize with the consciousness, as long as he had not died completely, there was still a slim possibility of a comeback.

Even if he could not make a comeback, he could at least live for a while longer and pass on his cultivation techniques to someone.

In this remote wilderness, he would not be able to cultivate to great heights, but as long as he consumed blood and essence, he could still raise his cultivation to the fifth or sixth rank.

Then he could cross the great mountains and return to the inner domain.

The descendants of the Blood Spirit Sect could still avenge him.

But this young man was truly hateful. After killing him, he was still intent on grinding the bones and scattering the ashes!

Even though the person had been cleaved in two and was thoroughly dead, he still would not relent!

However, Meng Chong did not give him any chance. His eyes gleamed with cold light, deeply impressed by the precious martial experience imparted by his master.

If it had been someone else, they might have thought the enemy was dead and simply turned and left.

"Die!"

Meng Chong, fearing the enemy might have some sinister tricks, brought down his sword, the tyrannical sword intent suppressing everything.

Poof, the worm was completely crushed.

"No..." a faint cry of unwillingness sounded, and then it disappeared without a trace.

Meng Chong still did not dare to be careless. The sword light swept across the battlefield, the sword intent surging, his blazing qi and blood covering the entire area, crushing everything in its path.

Not even a single blade of grass was spared.

The corpse was completely reduced to ashes, impossible to find, and the mysterious worm had also met the same fate.

In the end, only a few silver-white grasses remained!

Meng Chong panted, carefully approaching and using the tip of his sword to lift the grasses, examining them closely to ensure there were no issues. He then tore off a piece of his already tattered clothes and wrapped the silver-white grasses in it.

However, he did not keep them on his person, instead carrying them on the tip of his sword.

After the incident with the Blood Spirit Sect Patriarch, Meng Chong had become even more cautious. He glanced at the Wu capital, deciding not to go there, in case there were still powerful enemies lurking.

He looked at the serrated sword left behind by the Blood Spirit Sect Patriarch and retrieved it with a wave of his hand.

It seemed that after the battle and killing the enemy, he had directly returned without entering the Wu capital.

His brow slightly furrowed, as he found no trace of any martial artists.

Could it be that there was only one martial artist?

And he had already been killed by Meng Chong!

Evidently, the formidable enemy was only one.

What kind of enemy had Meng Chong faced?

Could it be someone from the inner realm?

He would have to wait for Meng Chong to return to know the details.
