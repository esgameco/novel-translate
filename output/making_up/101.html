Chapter 101: Su Lingxiu's Insights, the Treasure Trove of Medicine

Su Lingxiu immersed herself completely in comprehending the principles of medicine, spending most of her time in her room organizing her insights and trying to formulate medicinal prescriptions based on the theory of "monarch, minister, assistant, and envoy".

Li Xuan would occasionally check on her, marveling at how this girl, who had started studying medicine at age 4 and treated patients at 6, had already written out the characteristics and effects of various precious medicines in such a short time, and had even begun to create her own medicinal formulas.

"The theoretical aspects of medicine pose no problem for her. The current difficulty lies in how to gain insights into the cultivation methods and enter the path of martial arts.

"Otherwise, no matter how much she comprehends about medicine, without a foundation in martial arts, she will be limited in her ability to refine any potent elixirs."

The core of the medicine-martial arts cultivation lies not in medicine or martial arts themselves, but in the martial arts. Using martial arts to drive the medical arts - this is the true essence, the fundamental principle.

Apart from organizing her insights on medicine, Su Lingxiu also had Stone Two find a human-sized puppet and meticulously marked all the acupuncture points on it.

Li Xuan was increasingly satisfied, knowing that once Su Lingxiu succeeded in entering the path of cultivation, she would be able to map out all 720 acupuncture points on the body.

"When Xu Yan returns, I'll have him guide this disciple sister. With his insights and imagination, he should be able to assist Su Lingxiu in comprehending the cultivation methods."

Li Xuan made up his mind. Su Lingxiu's talent in the Dao of medicine was truly monstrous. It would be a shame if she could not enter the path of martial arts.

While organizing her insights on medicine and formulating medicinal prescriptions, Su Lingxiu was also contemplating the cultivation methods. Compared to her profound understanding of medicine, her insights into cultivation were not as great.

But she was not anxious. Once she finished organizing her work on medicine, she would devote herself wholeheartedly to comprehending the cultivation methods. She was confident that with her talents, she would be able to grasp them.

Stone Two was somewhat troubled, as he was stuck at the stage of refining the dantian, unable to break through to the stage of "sinews and bones rumbling, qi and blood surging". He could only continue to cultivate diligently, researching how to break through.

Li Xuan did not intervene, as Stone Two's talent was still acceptable. Breaking through the qi and blood realm was just a matter of time.

A golden light flashed.

"Your disciple Xu Yan has broken through to the initial stage of the Xiantian Realm. You have reached the Xiantian Realm Perfection."

Xu Yan had broken through to the initial stage of the Xiantian Realm, and his cultivation speed was remarkably fast, living up to his status as the pioneer of Li Xuan's martial arts legacy.

Li Xuan was delighted, as his strength had increased once again.

The next day, Xu Yan finally returned.

Li Xuan was happy to see his senior disciple back, but decided to hold off on passing down the cultivation methods beyond the Xiantian Realm for now. Instead, he wanted Xu Yan to assist Su Lingxiu in comprehending the cultivation methods of the medicine-martial arts path.

If Xu Yan were to focus on the advanced cultivation methods, his attention would be divided, and he might not be able to devote enough effort to helping Su Lingxiu.

With Xu Yan's monstrous talent as a Xiantian Realm cultivator, he should be able to help Su Lingxiu comprehend the cultivation methods in a relatively short time.

Although the final insights would not be entirely Su Lingxiu's own, as Xu Yan would be assisting her, the cultivation itself would still be hers. Li Xuan believed she should still be able to receive the recognition and feedback from the "golden finger".

After the introductions, Su Lingxiu eagerly asked Xu Yan for guidance on her cultivation doubts, remembering Li Xuan's words about seeking help from her senior disciple.

Xu Yan readily agreed, and Li Xuan was satisfied, seeing that Xu Yan carried himself with the bearing of a senior disciple.

The three disciples then left the courtyard and headed outside the city, where Xu Yan and Stone Two reunited. Su Lingxiu then began to explain the cultivation methods of the medicine-martial arts path.

Meng Chong listened with a furrowed brow, his expression blank, feeling that this cultivation method was beyond his innate talents. Scratching his head, he chuckled awkwardly, "Sister, I'm afraid I won't be of much help."

"Second Brother, everyone has their own talents," Su Lingxiu replied, not surprised by his reaction.
