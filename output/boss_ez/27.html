Fang Yu realized something.

Under similar blood conditions, the people of the Fool's Underworld seemed to be stronger than the people of the martial arts school.

Like Lin Wu, who had over 30 blood but could only unleash 7 ox-like force.

If he were in Xian Qianxuan's team, he would have been killed by the demons long ago, not even qualified to join the team.

As for Fang Yu himself, he had thrown over a dozen punches, each one with full force, and had gradually mastered the most suitable punching posture.

The only problem was that he was a bit exhausted.

So now whenever others called him to punch, he would just wave his hand, refusing to do it, sitting down to rest and recover his strength.

His physique couldn't keep up.

This stamina, he was afraid that halfway through a fight, he would simply run out of energy.

Something was wrong, very wrong.

Fang Yu consulted Lin Wu beside him.

"It's quite normal," Lin Wu said.

"For a punch like yours with full force, it's actually quite difficult to pull off in actual combat. Because it requires preparatory movements, and the opponent also needs to give you that opportunity."

"Therefore, in my daily sparring, my upper limit can reach 7 ox-like force, but in actual combat it's only around 3-5 ox-force."

"The significance of accumulating martial arts and combat experience is to give us more opportunities to find the chance to unleash a full-powered strike."

"As for you getting exhausted after a dozen punches, in actual combat you won't even have the chance to do that many, so your stamina should generally be enough."

"Moreover, we also need to cultivate our qi and blood every day, so our physical fitness and physique are constantly improving."

Fang Yu understood.

Combat is not like hitting a wooden post.

The enemy will dodge, evade, and block.

There won't be that many opportunities for full-powered punches.

So the stamina is sufficient, and the damage will also fluctuate.

At the same time, improving one's physique can increase stamina, allowing for a few more full-powered punches.

Fang Yu looked at the system prompt records.

[System prompt: Player diligently practices "Primal Body Stabilization Technique", proficiency +1.]

[System prompt: Player diligently practices "Primal Body Stabilization Technique", proficiency +1.]

[System prompt: Player diligently practices "Primal Body Stabilization Technique", proficiency +1, experience +1.]

Just three prompts.

After throwing over a dozen punches and almost collapsing from exhaustion, it only triggered three prompts.

A total of proficiency +3, experience +1.

After mastering the "Primal Body Stabilization Technique", there's no need to guide the qi anymore.

Instead, it's about mobilizing one's own qi and blood.

The initial qi guidance was just to stimulate the entire body's qi and blood, triggering a certain physical transformation.

Afterwards, by mobilizing the qi and blood, engaging in large-scale movements, and exhausting the physical strength, one can incidentally cultivate the "Primal Body Stabilization Technique" and increase its proficiency.

Opened the attribute panel.

[Name: Diao Deyi]

[Realm: Initial Flower Realm]

[Health: 22/22]

[Physique: 2.2] + [Spirit: 1.7] + [Skills: Digital Life [Human], Primal Body Stabilization Technique [Flower/Slight Mastery (3/300)]+]

[Attribute Points: 2]

[Experience: 19/100]

At this moment, Fang Yu finally understood the lament of those players on the forum.

The experience growth is truly slow.

Furthermore, the Spirit attribute, he had researched online, and most people said it was...useless.

Some speculated that Spirit might represent aptitude, martial arts comprehension, and cultivation efficiency.

But in reality, after some people allocated points to it, they concluded that it had no effect at all.

The Spirit attribute is like a waste attribute, having no impact on the character.

Far less practical and reliable than directly adding 10 points to Health.

At least in the early stage of the game, this seems to be the case.

The general conclusion is that the Spirit attribute is yet to be developed.

Translating it simply means: the future is promising.

Looking at the remaining 2 attribute points on the panel.

Fang Yu thought for a moment and made a decision.

All 2 attribute points will be allocated to the "Primal Body Stabilization Technique"!
