"Now that we have this clue, we should be able to find it soon."

The three exchanged a glance, then stuck to the edge of the abyss, quietly flying towards the depths of the Cangwu Abyss.

As they descended to about two hundred zhang above the ground, the scenery inside the abyss began to change drastically from the surface.

Due to the perennial presence of clouds and mist above, the further down they went, the darker the surrounding environment became.

The cliff walls were no longer bare rocks but now rooted with various strange plants.

There were luminescent willow vines, predatory pines disguised as rocks.

There were even withered black trees lurking like pythons, waiting to strike when prey approached.

And accompanying these plants were the peculiar beasts mentioned in the jade slip, which one needed to guard against.

These beasts were mostly pitch black, hard to perceive in the dim abyss.

Their appearances were grotesque, vastly different from the creatures on the surface.

They didn't seem naturally evolved but like fragments forcefully amalgamated from various animals.

There were long worms splitting down the middle, opening their mouths to prey.

There were strange apes with their heads tucked inside their bellies.

There were strange birds with wings made of countless wriggling centipedes.

...

Each unique form of these creatures gave a chilling feeling.

Moreover, in this abyss, there seemed to be a mysterious power.

It suppressed the perception range of the cultivators' divine sense.

This power became more pronounced as they delved deeper.

During the cultivators' descent, these bizarre creatures would suddenly emerge from the cliff walls or the void in the center of the abyss.

They would swarm and launch attacks on the cultivators.

As if harboring a deep hatred for foreign cultivators, these creatures would only cease their attacks upon complete annihilation.

Fortunately, although there were numerous beings in the abyss below, their power was equivalent to that of late Foundation Establishment stage cultivators.

For Su Xiaomei and her companions, all at least at the Golden Core stage, they did not pose much of a threat.

Having a safe journey, spending nearly half a day, they finally approached a massive boundary monument wedged in the cliff wall.

Emblazoned with bright red glowing characters on top were the words "Thirty Thousand".

It was visible from afar.

And above this boundary monument, the continuous cliff walls were almost hollowed inward.

Creating a glittering floating city above the abyss.

"This is the first resting point for exploring the Cangwu Abyss."

"Lincang City."

"Going further down, the abyss becomes increasingly dangerous. So, it's necessary to have a place here for rest and supplies."

Xiao Heng watched the unceasing cultivators passing by, silently raising his vigilance.

Most of the cultivators exploring the Cangwu Abyss were not easy opponents.

Besides gathering the valuable special products of the Cangwu Abyss, they mostly came for the meteorite that fell from the sky that year.

In recent years, the true identity of the meteorite had been a topic of varied speculations.

Whether it was the ancient divine cauldron that broke through the realms or some other widely circulated speculation.

Regardless, every cultivator who witnessed it felt a sense of awe.

This meteorite must be extraordinary.

As the saying goes, wealth leads to one's death.

So it was not difficult to understand why external cultivators tirelessly sought after it.

"Let's go." Xiao Heng and his group did not enter Lincang City.

They flew over the boundary monument and descended to the second layer beneath the earth's thirty thousand zhang.

Upon crossing the boundary monument, they seemed to pass through an invisible shield.

A peculiar sensation surged in Xiao Heng's mind.

"My strength has decreased by a bit, just as rumored."

Feeling somewhat heavy-hearted, Xiao Heng inquired with Su Xiaomei and Zhang Haobo.

"Yes, indeed, there's a slight decrease. But for now, the impact isn't significant," Su Xiaomei and Zhang Haobo replied after a moment.

"It seems my strength has weakened after all," Xiao Heng thought helplessly.

He could only hope that the location of the divine cauldron was within the depth range he could reach.

"The Power of Townsealing" was the general term used by cultivators for the inexplicable force enveloping the Cangwu Abyss.

The specific manifestation was that as one delved deeper into the abyss, a cultivator's strength would be correspondingly reduced.

In the first layer within thirty thousand zhang, it was not yet noticeable.

It merely interfered with the coverage range of their divine sense.

However, upon entering the second layer, the effect immediately became apparent.

Cultivators at the Qi Refinement Intermediate stage or below, once inside, would become no different from ordinary people.

Their spiritual power couldn't be mobilized, losing their ability to fly.

Their fate would only end in one way: falling helplessly towards the bottomless abyss, becoming food for the strange beings along the way.

This meant that at the very least, cultivators at the Foundation Establishment stage were required to survive in the Cangwu Abyss' second layer.

But in reality, as the depth increased, the inhabitants of the abyss also grew stronger.

Meanwhile, the cultivators' powers were continuously suppressed.

This led to it becoming harder to explore further down.

Xiao Heng and his companions proceeded deeper into the second layer, the "Forest of Lingering Voices," with increasing caution.

Trees deeply rooted in the abyss wall grew in defiance of logic, extending their branches willfully towards the void of the Cangwu Abyss at the center.

They formed an endless primitive forest occupying a space of fifty thousand zhang vertically.

Apart from the increasingly diverse and powerful dangerous creatures, the murmuring whispers emanating from the forest were even deadlier.

These whispers caused cultivators to experience various illusions, losing their mental faculties.

Those with weak wills would seem as if they had lost their souls, flying towards the center of the Forest of Lingering Voices, never to return.

Xiao Heng and his companions were all considered outstanding individuals with strong wills.

Combined with their formidable strength, it took them almost a day to traverse the second layer.

They descended to a depth of eighty thousand zhang.

At this point, the dense forest suddenly became sparse.

There seemed to be another light source below, and their vision cleared abruptly.

Instead of the towering stone monument as before, a huge eyeball growing on the cliff wall now marked the boundary layer.

The central pupil of the eyeball was replaced by the two blood-red characters "Eighty Thousand."

The eyeball seemed to be alive, constantly rotating and scanning.

Feeling someone approaching from above, it instantly fixed its gaze on Xiao Heng and his group.

A tremendous pressure bore down on Xiao Heng, making his body tense in an instant.

Fortunately, the giant eye glanced at them briefly and then averted its gaze, not looking further.

"Whew..."

Xiao Heng breathed a sigh of relief.
