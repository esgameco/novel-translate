The autumn dusk was not bleak, the golden sunlight was lazy yet warm.

Liu Sheng was dressed casually, smiling cheerfully, just like an ordinary old man.

How coincidental that this Qi Yuan from Ninguo appeared? He might have a special relationship with that revived deity.

Liu Chufeng suddenly remembered something and muttered, "I don't know if he's back yet, grandpa, let's go to his courtyard."

Liu Chufeng led Liu Sheng to Qi Yuan's courtyard.

A light knock on the door sounded, and Chen Kangbao poked his head out, looking curious at the two.

"Oh, I forgot to tell you, the young master is back." Chen Kangbao was still eating a steamed bun as usual.

"He's back, my grandpa wants to see him." Liu Chufeng said as she was about to walk into the courtyard.

Chen Kangbao stretched out his hand to stop Liu Chufeng: "The young master is sleeping, he has ordered that no one is allowed to go in."

"Sleeping?" Liu Chufeng became a little anxious when she heard this, "Wake him up!"

Her grandpa rarely came here in a good mood, this was a rare opportunity.

Who in the world doesn't want to see the divine military commander and get guidance from him? Chen Kangbao shook his head and said seriously, "The young master said not to wake him up."

He stood firmly in front of Liu Chufeng.

Liu Chufeng was very angry: "Delaying an important matter, you..."

"The young master's sleep is the important matter." Chen Kangbao said bluntly.

Liu Chufeng fell silent at this.

She felt sorry for Qi Yuan.

At this time, Liu Sheng smiled and said, "How long will your young master be asleep?"

"I don't know." Chen Kangbao shook his head.

Liu Chufeng was even more speechless when she heard this.

Liu Sheng glanced into the courtyard: "Since I have nothing to do, I'll just wait here."

Liu Chufeng heard this, her eyes filled with surprise and confusion.

Wait? Her grandpa wants to wait for Brother Qi to wake up? Liu Chufeng was puzzled.

She naturally didn't think it was for her own face that her grandpa was waiting.

Her grandpa was a divine military commander, even if he was her relative, she still needed to respect him.

Her grandpa must have his own reasons for doing things.

She lowered her head, not knowing what she was thinking, waiting patiently.

From dusk to deep night, Liu Chufeng and Liu Sheng waited outside the courtyard.

The door of the room never opened, Liu Chufeng's eyes were filled with confusion: "Brother Qi still hasn't woken up? When will he wake up?"

Chen Kangbao shook his head: "I don't know."

"It's okay, he'll wake up at the latest by tomorrow morning, grandpa you..." Liu Chufeng looked at Liu Sheng, a little afraid that her grandpa would get angry.

After all, keeping a divine military commander waiting outside until midnight, or even all night, this Qi Yuan was unprecedented.

Liu Sheng smiled, his smile was kind, he glanced at the house: "No hurry, I'm free now."

At this time, Chen Kangbao said, "The young master may not wake up tomorrow morning."

"Ah?" Liu Chufeng was stunned, "He's not injured, is he? How can an ordinary person sleep so long?"

"Hmph, the young master is a deity descended from heaven, what's wrong with sleeping for three thousand years?" Chen Kangbao said proudly.

Liu Chufeng fell silent, this Chen Kangbao was really sick.

And next to him, Liu Sheng narrowed his eyes, as if he had thought of something, scrutinizing Chen Kangbao's eyes.

After a long time, he smiled and said, "There is such an interesting thing in this world, I'll just wait here for your young master to wake up and see how many months he can sleep."

At this time, a young man in Confucian robes lowered his head, his face filled with fear: "Is this something we can hear?"

Although he had heard the story of the courtesan beheading the three princes since childhood.

But this Rakshasa country had long banned the spread of these stories.

The diners in the restaurant applauded approvingly.

"You must be an outsider, this news has already spread throughout the coastal city!"

"Hehe, the Deity of Judgment... has returned!"

"He... has finally returned." An old man in a tattered short jacket, drinking murky yellow wine.

"After encountering the courtesan..."

On the other side, in the corner, an old woman in cloth clothes, the sound in the restaurant reached her ears, and a reminiscent expression appeared on her face.

"I never thought that a hundred and twenty years have passed."

Although she was in coarse cloth clothes, she could not hide a special aura, and from her facial features, one could see that she was a beauty in her youth.

"Madam, it has been a hundred years since the deity disappeared, and we have finally waited for him to return." The speaker was a lame old man.

He was of humble origin, and was beaten and had his leg broken for not kneeling before a nobleman.

It was the deity who restored his justice and dignity.

As for the old woman, she was the protagonist of the "Courtesan Beheading the Three Princes" story, and was also one of the two deputy directors of the Judgment Hall.

Except for the director who was trapped in the capital and unable to get out, the other deputy director had to guard the Judgment Hall.

This time, with the news of the deity's appearance in the coastal city, the old woman came in person.

Her aged face was full of anticipation.

"The current Rakshasa country... is nothing compared to the past." The old woman said softly, her eyes filled with nostalgia.

As people get older, they tend to reminisce about the past.

The old woman was not living in the past! But even though the court had been suppressing the Judgment Hall and erasing the traces of the deity's existence, the people's stories and beliefs about the deity had never been interrupted.

Many people secretly worshipped the deity, just waiting for the day when the deity would reappear.

The entire court was lined up, with one person pulled out and beheaded every other person, among whom there might be innocent people, but certainly not a few with bloody hands.

The current Rakshasa country, how could it still be the original Rakshasa country? The royal family was willing to cede cities to ensure their position, and the generals were beheading civilians to gain military merit!
