"Let's go, we'll return to the Seven-Month Lake, and I should leave as well." Qi Yuan took the Moon Maiden's hand, and their figures disappeared into the vast snow.

The Moon Maiden looked at Qi Yuan in silence.

Qi Yuan closed his eyes after speaking.

At this time, on the Seven-Color Peak, Qi Yuan came out of the game.

"Junior sister."

"Junior sister, I'm preparing to cultivate the Heavenly Dao, so naturally it will take longer." Qi Yuan said seriously.

Jiang Lingsu was speechless.

Cultivating the Heavenly Dao? What nonsense.

Her big brother's combat power was so strong, it was probably related to cultivating many powerful secret techniques.

Qi Yuan gave a few more instructions, then returned to his own room.

He looked at the kitchen knife in the courtyard, bent down, and untied the rope around its neck: "I won't tie you up anymore, while I'm in seclusion, you'll have to watch the house for me."

The kitchen knife's body seemed to tremble a bit, as if agreeing.

Qi Yuan entered the house and set up a few small formations using the array jade slips he had taken from the Black Mountain Sect.

He then re-entered the game.

The cold snow fell on his face, and he opened his eyes to see a small, reddened hand carefully brushing the snow off his shoulder.

"Sir... you're awake?" The Moon Maiden saw that Qi Yuan had opened his eyes, and her small hand retracted.

She was going to bring out the fish soup she had made.

Before the sir left, she had to let him drink a sip of the fish soup she had made herself.

The Moon Maiden said this and hurried back into the hut.

"Willing to sacrifice much, bold enough to change heaven and earth."

The Moon Maiden was still holding the bowl, her footsteps lifted off the ground, not even realizing the fishing line in the sky.

Qi Yuan's sword was still slanted in the snow, and he was still sitting on the blue stone, watching the snowflakes fall and land on his hair.

The fishing line was very sharp, piercing through flesh and blood, moving so fast that there was no time for blood to flow out.

The bowl in the Moon Maiden's hands fell to the ground with a clang.
