Chapter 38

Here is the full English translation of the Chinese excerpt:

In her dream, she was running happily across the vast grasslands of Qingqiu, surrounded by her tribesmen, her younger sister, and her elder brother.

The fox was free.

As a member of the royal bloodline, she only needed to quietly consume the essence of the sun and moon, and she would become a great demon after a hundred years.

Until that day, when the skies over Qingqiu changed.

The elders on the throne and her own siblings fought against each other.

The lush and vibrant Qingqiu instantly turned into a barren, blood-soaked land.

As young as they were, they could only flee.

The tribesmen around them grew fewer and fewer, either dying on the road or being captured...

In the end, only she and her sister crossed the endless mountains, arriving at a remote human domain, the Great Yan.

She and her sister got into an argument.

She wanted to live peacefully, cultivate quietly, and perhaps one day return to Qingqiu, nothing more.

Her sister did not want that. She wanted power, she wanted to go back to Qingqiu and kill all those who had brought them to this state.

For this, her sister did not hesitate to kill people and take their essence, so that her cultivation could advance faster.

"Humans kill us, so we'll eat humans. What's wrong with that?"

"Sister, I think you've lost your mind. If that's your mindset, I might as well eat you now, rather than let you be tormented by humans later."

Bailu could feel her sister's sincere murderous intent, so the two went their separate ways.

Thanks to the illusionary techniques of the fox demons, as well as her own diligence, she joined a human sect to cultivate.

At first, Bailu thought humans were afraid of demons because demons ate people.

But after these years in the Great Yan, she found that some humans were more terrifying than demons.

Bailu began to crave power.

Images flashed through her mind, and in the end, she found her sister in the Yunwu Mountains.

Their relationship was no longer as it was before. What Bailu wanted was an opportunity, the Thousand Illusion Purple Gold Bell.

Bailu didn't actually want to kill her, but later, she was killed by a prison guard from the Celestial Prison.

Finally, she heard the sound of the bell.

"Hey, wake up, wake up."

"Look, her flesh... it's growing back, and her wounds have healed."

"You crazy bastard, you actually managed to revive her even after she died."

So noisy.

The dream dissipated, and Bailu's consciousness gradually became clear.

She felt that she was missing a tail...

She knew what this meant.

"So I died once... was that a near-death experience?" Bailu thought to herself.

She then slowly got up from the ground, her gaze somewhat weary as she looked around.

The village...

The memories from earlier were also in her mind, and she was surprised and fearful.

Weren't all the people in this village killed that night? Why was she here, thinking she was a villager? It was as if her entire perception had been altered.

If she hadn't died once, she truly wouldn't have remembered those things.

Looking at the people around her, Bailu pondered for a moment, then made up her mind.

Since she had regained her memories, she would now try to find a way to leave this place. As for this mysterious village, it would be best not to stir up any trouble.

For now, she would just pretend that everything was normal.

Thinking this, she forced a weak smile onto her face and gently said to the people around her, "Thank you all for your concern, but I'm fine now. You can all go."

Xurang's rough voice came from the side.

"Little Bailu, I'm glad you're okay, but you should thank that crazy guy. If it weren't for him, you wouldn't be alive."

"Yes, yes, you should really thank the crazy guy."

The crazy guy? Was that the strange and erratic prison guard? And his methods were quite bizarre before.

Now that the person is a lunatic, should she take the opportunity to kill him and then leave the village?

Here is my translation of the Chinese excerpt:

Her gaze turned towards Ye Wuyou, who was standing beside her at the moment, and judging by his expression, he seemed to be intently gazing at her.

Hmph, a madman... What are you looking at, is it really that good-looking?

Bai Lu was very confident in her own appearance. As a fox demon from the royal clan, whether as a demon or as a human, she was the most dazzling beauty in the world.

However, just as she was thinking this to herself, the person in front of her suddenly opened his mouth and responded.

"I'm looking at your tail, at first there were three, tsk, now there are only two, hey, what happened to the other one, did I see it wrong?"

Ye Wuyou rubbed his eyes and then walked closer, squatting down.

His gaze was directed towards the edge of the white dress, as if he wanted to see where the tail was and how it grew out.

A fierce gust of wind passed by his ear, but then abruptly stopped.

"You madman, what are you trying to do to the young lady of the Bai family? Even if you saved someone else, it won't work."

Ye Wuyou turned his head, and there was a palm in front of his face.

It had stopped right next to his cheek, seemingly about to slap him.

But for some reason, it had stopped.

His gaze swept across the hem of the dress, across the palm, and then up to the towering mountains, finally resting on the angry yet still beautiful face.

"Could it be that you really don't have a tail..."

Before he could finish speaking, a shrill scream came from the woman.

Her voice was full of anger and bewilderment!

"Why, why do you have the techniques of the Demon Beast Sect?

Why! Why am I your spirit beast?"

Bai Lu's eyes revealed a trace of desolation, her expression filled with deep confusion.

She and the Ye Wuyou in front of her, for some unknown reason, had developed a connection.

As a fox demon, she was very clear about the origin of this connection.

Seeing Bai Lu so agitated, the nearby Lin Qingqing hurriedly supported her, and then the surrounding people started discussing in a clamor.

"What was Bai Miss just saying? I didn't understand at all."

"Oh no, could it be that after being saved by the madman, she's also become a madwoman?"

"Bai Miss, don't be angry, calm down first. Just now you even stopped breathing, it was the madman who saved you."

Bai Lu's figure almost started trembling, and a tear glistened at the corner of her beautiful face.

Saved me?

This is an innate divine ability of the fox demons of the Qingqiu royal clan, one tail for one life.

What does this have to do with him? And isn't he a prison guard of the Heavenly Prison? Why does he have the inheritance of the Demon Beast Sect's spirit beast techniques?

I would have just slept for a while after dying once, but now I've woken up and become someone else's spirit beast?

Ye Wuyou just glanced at her, not seeming to understand the meaning of her words, and then buried his head again, carefully examining the dress.

Tail, tail...

Then he was forcibly separated by someone.

"That's enough, madman. Although saving a life means the other person should offer themselves in return, in your case, you can only be a beast of burden in your next life..."

But then, Lin Qingqing covered her chin and looked Ye Wuyou up and down, letting out a soft exclamation.

"But if you can cure your madness, and you look not bad, the Miss might consider offering herself to you."

The usually silent Bai Changzhen, who was holding a book, added a finishing blow from the side.

"No way."

Everyone's gaze turned to him, with puzzlement in their eyes.

"You see, the Miss of the Bai family is also mad, and Ye Wuyou is a madman too. I think they... are quite well-matched."

Bai Lu's figure trembled, still immersed in this reality that she could not accept.

Hearing these words, her eyes darkened even more, and she angrily retorted.

"I'm not a madwoman, you all, you villagers who can't see the truth, you are the madmen!"

However, as soon as she said these words, the gazes of the crowd suddenly paused, and then they all turned to look at Ye Wuyou, silently nodding to each other.

Bai Changzhen, who was holding a book bag, shook his head slightly and sighed with deep meaning.

"See, she's still saying she's not a madwoman, even her words are the same."
