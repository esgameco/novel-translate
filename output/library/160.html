Ye Xiao quickly smelted the pig iron into a sub-transcendent-level weapon.

As long as he passed the assessment, there was no need to draw too much attention.

When the real competition begins, he will then reveal a bit of his true strength.

Apart from that, there is nothing else to worry about.

Soon, Ye Xiao obtained the competition participation certificate by passing the assessment.

He used a fake identity, and with the help of Taixuan Tiangong, he simply modified the assessor's memory to get everything settled.

After obtaining the participation certificate, he had to wait until the next day to enter the competition arena for the formal competition.

The competition mechanism was a single-elimination format, with one match determining the winner and loser, without any preliminary rounds or multiple stages of elimination leading up to the final championship.

All the participants had to enter the arena at the same time, then forge a weapon or armor, which would be judged and scored by a panel of nationally renowned forging masters.

The forging materials would be provided by the hosting organization, but participants could also bring their own.

This was to accommodate some forgers who were relatively specialized in certain areas.

Even if a forger was specialized, as long as they could reach a certain standard in their area of expertise, they would be considered an exceptional talent, and the rigid competition rules should not restrict their performance and cause the Nine Provinces to miss out on such talents.

Of course, the hosting organization did not need to worry about some participants bringing expensive rare materials to gain an unfair advantage, because the more precious the material, the higher the technical requirement for forging it. If a forger's skills were not up to par, the item forged with high-end materials would actually be inferior to what they could forge with low-end materials.

The materials provided by the hosting organization were at an appropriate level - not too difficult, nor too easy.

As a result, very few forgers would bring their own materials.

The competition results were determined by both the quality of the forged item and the forging time.

If participants brought their own materials, points would be added or deducted based on the quality of those materials.

As for the rewards, the top 1,000 competitors in the forging competition would all receive prizes.

This was because the number of participants was extremely large, as the population of the Nine Provinces was immense, and even though the proportion of forgers and alchemists was small, the total number was still staggering. The number of participants each year was at least over 10,000, and this was with an age limit in place - otherwise, the number would be even higher.

The prizes were distributed according to the rankings, and Ye Xiao was only interested in the top prizes, which included: a core of a peak Saint-Emperor-level beast, a pair of claws of a peak Saint-Emperor-level beast, 3 jin of rare spatial purple steel, a piece of special blood wood material, and a block of spatial purple crystal.

Ye Xiao was not particularly interested in the competition rules, as he was confident in his forging abilities. While his combat strength at the early Transcendent Eighth Realm was lacking, in terms of forging skills, there were likely not many in this world who could easily forge imperial-grade weapons.

What surprised him was that the spatial purple crystal was only the fifth-place prize, but upon reflection, it made sense, as forging spatial materials, though excellent, was not an easy task and required exceptional forging skills. Most participants under the age of 30 would not have such high-level skills.

This was perfect for Ye Xiao, as the challenge would be even simpler for him. He had decided to forge an exceptional weapon and claim the spatial purple crystal as his prize, without drawing too much attention to himself.

Arriving at a secluded small forest on the outskirts of the city, Ye Xiao began processing the several Saint-Emperor-level beasts he had captured. He had not yet revived them, but had sealed their souls within himself, planning to revive and raise them after returning to Jianghai City.

Now, Ye Xiao intended to extract their spirit pearls and use the Taixuan Tiangong to analyze their imperial arts, in order to obtain the ones he needed.

Among them were a chicken, a fish, a turtle, a deer, and a ginseng. All were Ye Xiao's favorite foods.

The turtle, deer, and ginseng were also highly nourishing.

Although Ye Xiao no longer needed such nourishment, having them was still much better than not.

Moreover, their true value lay in their ability to enhance cultivation and vitality, which was far superior to the other types.

However, Ye Xiao was somewhat disappointed after analyzing the spirit pearls through the Taixuan Tiangong, as there were only two imperial arts - one was the Earth Escape Art belonging to the ginseng beast's Immortal Elder.
