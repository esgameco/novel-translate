Any Titanium-Cobalt Dragon would at least become a super Anomaly in the future, with unparalleled strength and status.

Compared to Ranger, the gap between the two is like heaven and earth.

"I didn't apply, I just changed my plan," Jing Xinghuos didn't explain much.

Lu Yunhai could tell he didn't want to go into details, so he didn't pry further, and smiled, "Ranger is good too, no need to worry about employment."

Ranger's main weapon is the bow and arrow. A Ranger must master archery, and practice it diligently, at least until they become a super Anomaly.

In his sophomore year, Lu Yunhai tried to fuse with an alien species and became a "Hunter", a profession similar to Ranger that also uses the bow and arrow as the main weapon, and has a battle pet.

Unfortunately, Lu Yunhai's fusion failed. But he didn't give up, and after recovering, he started working part-time outside of school to save money for a second fusion attempt, which he continued until now.

After graduation, Lu Yunhai officially became a coach at the archery hall.

Jing Xinghuos wanted to learn archery, and naturally thought of his good friend first, plus the location was very close to home, so he wanted to help increase Lu Yunhai's business.

When Lu Yunhai heard that Jing Xinghuos wanted to learn archery, he was very proud.

Jing Xinghuos was a bit annoyed, "You have to give me a discount."

"Okay, okay, I'll give you the best price," Lu Yunhai teased enough, and his mood was relaxed. "Follow me."

He led Jing Xinghuos into the archery hall. Along the way, they ran into colleagues and students, many of whom called Lu Yunhai "Coach Lu", showing that he had a high status in the archery hall.

"You're doing well," Jing Xinghuos teased.

"Of course." Lu Yunhai boasted, "Although I'm not an Anomaly yet, in terms of archery skills, I can rank in the top five, or even compete for the top three in our hall, only the hall director can solidly beat me."

Jing Xinghuos was curious, "The hall director is very skilled?"

"Definitely!" Lu Yunhai said admiringly, "Our hall director is also a Ranger, but he's a super Anomaly. Too many people want to learn archery from him, even Anomalies, and he charges over 10,000 Aya per lesson, but the class schedule is snatched up as soon as it's released."

Jing Xinghuos nodded.

For any super Anomaly, no matter what job they do, their income is astronomical. The public data from the Star Division shows that the number of super Anomalies in the Asia-Pacific Community has exceeded 15 million, while the total national population is 56 billion. The proportion of super Anomalies is so low, which shows how rare and valuable they are.

Any super Anomaly can easily make money.

Lu Yunhai quickly registered Jing Xinghuos as a member, and said, "The courses and prices are different for different bow types. Which one do you want to learn, traditional, light, or compound?"

"Compound."

Jing Xinghuos didn't think much before giving the answer, not even asking about the price difference.

Lu Yunhai nodded unsurprisingly.

This is the choice of most Rangers, for a simple reason - the compound bow has the greatest power, fastest arrow speed, and highest accuracy.

Traditional bows and light bows have some advantages, but it is difficult to make up for the huge power difference with the compound bow.

There is also a type of competitive recurve bow, but it is too complex, inconvenient to carry, and tedious to maintain, so it is almost never chosen by Rangers, and is only used by hobbyists in sports competitions, a very niche market.

As for the light bow, it is the recurve bow with most of the accessories removed.

Some traditional bows also belong to the recurve category.

"The introductory course for the compound bow is 20 lessons, one lesson per day, for a total of 6,000 Aya."

Lu Yunhai lowered his voice, "I'll give you a 30% discount."

"Okay, thank you," Jing Xinghuos replied.

Lu Yunhai selected a shooting position and asked, "I'll go get the bow. Do you want a hunting bow or a target bow?"

Compound bows are further divided into two types - hunting bows and target bows. The two have no structural difference, but hunting bows are smaller in size for easier movement in the wild environment like forests and tunnels, with higher draw weights and greater power, while target bows are larger in size, suitable for open areas, with slightly lower draw weights for better accuracy.

Of course, the techniques for the two types of compound bows are mostly the same, and there are also many hybrid bow models on the market that cater to both uses.

Jiang Xinghuо was familiar with these basic archery knowledge.

"I want a hunting bow," he said.

Lu Yunhai then asked, "What draw weight?"

"70 kilograms," Jiang Xinghuо replied after estimating.

Draw weight refers to the amount of force required to draw the bow. The higher the draw weight, the greater the power. However, over a century ago, the unit was changed from "pounds" to the more commonly used "kilograms".

70 kilograms is equivalent to about 155 pounds.

"Damn it!" Lu Yunhai exclaimed enviously.

A compound hunting bow with such a high draw weight would be extremely difficult for an average person to draw, requiring years of practice and a very strong physique. Yet Jiang Xinghuо, a complete beginner, was using a bow of this magnitude.

Lu Yunhai himself had been practicing archery for years and only used a 60-kilogram bow.

"Anomalies are just amazing," he muttered.

Accustomed to interacting with anomalies at the archery hall, Lu Yunhai quickly went to fetch a compound bow, "Let me adjust the parameters first."

To bring out a bow's optimal performance, it must be matched to the shooter's build.

First, the draw length.

After adjusting the draw weight and sighting parameters, Lu Yunhai handed the bow to Jiang Xinghuо, "Try it out and see if it fits."

Jiang Xinghuо took the bow.

This compound hunting bow had an axle-to-axle length of 85 centimeters, an aluminum alloy riser, the typical perforated structure, and carbon fiber limbs. The entire bow weighed less than 3 kilograms, feeling light in his hands despite the high draw weight.

Although ranger bows sacrifice many accessories, this compound bow still retained a five-pin sight, a rest, a stabilizer, a string stop, and a cable guard - compact and durable components that greatly assist in accurate shooting.

The compound bow's single string and dual cables distribute the force evenly, and as the draw approached the limit, the eccentric cams at the limb tips reduced the required holding force by 80%, making it much easier and steadier.

The "peep sight" on the bowstring was now aligned with his right eye, forming a straight line with the second pin of the sight and the distant target.
