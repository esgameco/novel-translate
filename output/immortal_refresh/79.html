This side, the crowd around Chu Ning was in an uproar. Such a big commotion naturally also drew the attention of several other nearby shops.

Some cultivators who heard something, also couldn't help but inquire about it from Luo Yiping, Gu Xiaoqing and others.

Luo Yiping disdained to say anything, if someone asked, he would just give a light "hmm" in response.

However, Gu Xiaoqing patiently explained, "This Junior Brother Chu is a disciple of our Qingxi Sect's Baiyi Hall and Lingfu Pavilion, managing these two shops all by himself."

The Qingxi Sect actually had a young disciple managing two halls simultaneously, one person in charge of two shops.

The crowd originally surrounding Chu Ning's shop, after receiving this information, went from disbelieving to discussing it fervently.

Even some people in front of the neighboring shops of Luo Yiping, Gu Xiaoqing and others, also turned to go watch at Chu Ning's side.

However, it was just watching.

It was unknown whether it was because Chu Ning was too young,

Or because this dual-hall disciple surprisingly only had the fourth level of Qi refining, causing doubts about his abilities.

Not a single person came forward to inquire about making purchases.

From afar, Luo Yiping saw this and lightly laughed.

Especially when an hour passed, people gradually left Chu Ning's shop front, but not a single person came to inquire.

Luo Yiping, watching from not far away, sneered repeatedly.

He transmitted his voice to Shen Zhengquan and Gu Xiaoqing, "Now you see, no one is even willing to enter, this Junior Brother Chu is really quite capable."

Shen Zhengquan shook his head, not knowing what he meant.

Gu Xiaoqing did not respond, only slightly furrowing her brow.

At this moment, Chu Ning's brow also unconsciously creased slightly.

Earlier, he had purposely taken a turn around the market street, mainly to check out the talisman selling area of Wind Gorge.

In order to understand the situation more clearly, he even specially asked someone to help buy a few talismans.

After looking over the talismans he bought, Chu Ning gained great confidence.

For the same low-grade beginner talismans, the quality of his was definitely better than theirs.

After all, his innate spirit root talent brought tangible improvements to talisman quality during the forging process.

So for pricing, Chu Ning chose to directly match their rates.

Higher quality talismans, but the same price - buyers would naturally know how to choose.

However, even good wine fears deep alleys.

No matter how good these talismans were, if they didn't sell, their value couldn't be realized.

"Looks like I need to employ some tactics." Chu Ning inwardly shook his head.

Originally, he just wanted to make money quietly, without being too high-profile or showy.

But if the shop had no income, that wouldn't work.

According to the sect's rules, the Lingfu Pavilion shop had to hand over 500 spirit stones per month.

The Spiritual Plant House was lower at 200 spirit stones.

Added together, that was 700 spirit stones.

If he didn't open the shop, he couldn't afford that cost.

Thinking this, Chu Ning took out a sign he had prepared earlier from his storage pouch and wrote a line in large characters on it:

"Opening Day Special, Buy Five Talismans Get One Free!"

Then, with a Controlling Objects Spell, he directly hung this extremely eye-catching sign outside the shop.

For the reincarnated Chu Ning, he had too many promotional tactics to use.

And now, he had just casually chosen the simplest one.

The facts proved that Chu Ning's move was indeed effective - shortly after hanging the sign, a man looking to be around thirty years old, with the appearance of a wandering cultivator, walked into the shop after reading Chu Ning's sign and came up to him.

"Shopkeeper, it says here 'Buy Five Get One Free' for your talismans - does that mean buy five talismans and get one free?"

Chu Ning nodded, "Correct, if Fellow Daoist buys five talismans of the same price, I can give you one for free."

"The talisman quality is guaranteed, right?" The man asked hesitantly.

Chu Ning directly took out a talisman and handed it over to him.

"Produced by the Qingxi Sect, the quality is naturally assured."

The man took the talisman and...

He took it and looked at it, revealing a hint of delight on his face.

"The quality of these talisman papers is indeed not bad, it seems even better than those from Wind Gorge."

Chu Ning smiled without answering.

The man put down the talisman papers, and then asked Chu Ning:

"What if my companion and I buy together, but he wants to buy defensive talisman papers, is that possible?"

"Group purchases are supported," Chu Ning continued smiling.

"If my friend wants to buy defensive talisman papers, naturally he can combine them with the offensive ones. If you buy four 2 spirit stone talisman papers and one 3 spirit stone talisman paper, I can still give you one 2 spirit stone talisman paper for free."

Upon hearing this, the man immediately turned and called over another slightly older man.

After a brief discussion, they quickly decided and bought two Fire Talismans, one Profound Ice Talisman, one Rejuvenation Talisman, and one Earth Armor Talisman.

Chu Ning also let them pick another Fire Talisman as a gift.

The two men left excitedly with the talisman papers in hand.

With these two men making purchases, everyone else also understood what was going on.

Immediately, the discussions around grew louder.

Especially when the man just mentioned that the quality of Chu Ning's talisman papers was actually higher than those from Wind Gorge.

Many people were extremely surprised.

Afterwards, one or two more people came to inquire about purchasing.

However, they did not buy as many as five, only scattered purchases.

But for those frequently dealing with talisman papers, they could still clearly sense that the quality of these was indeed very high.

For a time, it made many people extremely surprised.

The market was not particularly large, and the talisman papers from the Qingxi Talisman Stall had higher quality than Wind Gorge Talisman Stall, plus buying more earned free gifts.

This news quickly spread through the market streets.

...

In the middle of the Yan Ji Market Street, at this moment, a gray-robed youth of twenty-four or twenty-five years old was frequently looking around.

When he encountered lone cultivators, he would take the initiative to approach and inquire about something.

However, several people in a row ignored him.

Finally, when he asked a beautiful female cultivator in her thirties, she stopped.

"Is what you said true? The talisman papers from the Qingxi Talisman Stall really have higher quality than Wind Gorge, and buying five earns a free one?"

The beautiful lady looked at the gray-robed youth.

The youth immediately responded, "It's true, I just went to take a look. I want to buy 3 talisman papers. Does my friend plan to buy any? If possible, we can combine our purchases. For 3 talisman papers, we can save 1 low-grade spirit stone!"

After listening, the lady seemed moved and finally nodded, "Alright, let's go take a look then."

Upon hearing this, the gray-robed youth immediately revealed a delighted expression.

He led the lady to Chu Ning's talisman stall, and after inquiring, they each took out 5 spirit stones.

Then they happily left with 3 talisman papers each.

After all, before this, although talisman papers were not rare in major sects, they were still quite popular in Yan Ji Market.

To have a situation where buying talisman papers earned free gifts was almost unheard of.

After a few transactions, the news of Chu Ning's talisman stall promotion spread even wider.

Almost every so often, cultivators would come to make purchases.

Moreover, it was mostly groups combining their purchases.

The Qingxi Talisman Stall's business had quietly become the hottest in the market.

Seeing the steady stream of people at Chu Ning's stall and his decent talisman paper sales, the other members of Qingxi Sect were quite surprised.

Even Luo Yiping, who originally looked at Chu Ning with cold disregard, was slightly taken aback.

The traffic at their few stalls was normal, but compared to Chu Ning's, it was far less.
