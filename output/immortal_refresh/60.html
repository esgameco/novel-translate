Chapter 60

Here is my full translation of the excerpt from Chinese to English:

It was still the practice ground from five days ago. Chu Ning arrived a little earlier this time.

At this moment, there were only more than twenty people inside the practice ground.

Looking at the expressions of these people, Chu Ning could roughly tell that they were mostly relaxed.

He estimated that those who arrived first probably had at least achieved the 20% success rate for talisman-making, which was the threshold to qualify.

Afterwards, more people gradually arrived, with most of their faces full of disappointment.

It was evident that even a 20% success rate in talisman-making was still too high for most people.

Soon, more and more people gathered.

The other five who were promoted along with Chu Ning also arrived, each with different expressions.

Luo Hongping and Lu Yunfang had normal expressions, making it hard to tell their situation.

Lu Ming and Chen Jinhua had slight looks of disappointment on their faces.

It was clear that they either failed to reach the 20% success rate or just barely passed but had little hope.

As for Jiang Dawei, he hung his head dejectedly, looking utterly dispirited, making his result self-evident.

Soon after, He Changyou appeared again in the practice ground, accompanied by the supervising disciple with the surname Liu.

He Changyou stood in front and, after glancing over the crowd, slowly spoke:

"The five-day period has ended. Those who have achieved a 20% success rate in making the Wood Talisman, step forward."

Immediately, a small portion of people from various parts of the practice ground stood up and walked towards the front.

Chu Ning waited for a moment but found that none of his five companions seemed to have any intention of getting up.

It was then that he slowly stood up and, under their astonished gazes, walked forward from the back.

"Chu... he..."

Jiang Dawei, who had been hanging his head dejectedly, now lifted his head slightly, his mouth slightly agape, but he failed to complete his sentence.

The other four also revealed surprised expressions on their faces.

It was not until Chu Ning walked to the very front of the practice ground that Lu Yunfang softly said:

"I didn't expect that this Junior Brother Chu not only has a talent for cultivating Wood-type spells but also a talent for talisman-making."

At this moment, Lu Ming asked enviously:

"Do you think he might be able to obtain one of the top ten spots and gain the qualification to learn talisman scriptures?"

The others did not answer, for no one could.

After five days of learning talisman-making, they had already realized that the talent for it had little relation to one's cultivation level.

Everyone present had enough spiritual power to easily draw dozens of talismans, but how many would ultimately succeed was hard to say.

"Twenty-seven people, not bad, a few more than I expected," said He Changyou, his gaze sweeping over the people standing in front.

When he saw Chu Ning standing at the very back, he was slightly surprised as well.

He clearly remembered that Chu Ning had just been promoted from the D-rank not long ago.

What He Changyou did not expect was that Chu Ning also seemed to have a talent for talisman-making.

However, this expression of surprise was only fleeting.

Soon, He Changyou's expression returned to normal.

"The other disciples, move back and clear the front of the practice ground.

Next, we will conduct a talisman-making test. Each person will draw ten Wood Talismans on talisman papers.

Those with success rates ranking in the top ten will officially gain the qualification to learn talisman scriptures."

After speaking, He Changyou had everyone spread out, with one table between each person to avoid interference.

Then, the supervising disciple with the surname Liu distributed ten talisman papers to each person.

Once everyone had taken out their talisman brushes and inks from their storage bags, He Changyou scanned them with his gaze.

"Begin!"

Following He Changyou's words, more than twenty people picked up their talisman brushes.

Chu Ning calmly held his brush, took a light breath, and slowly infused it with spiritual power.

For him, the Wood Talisman was already extremely familiar.

As long as he used his spiritual sense to perceive the talisman-making process, he had a 70% chance of success.

However, at this moment, Chu Ning did not choose to extend his spiritual sense outward.

After all, He Changyou, at the Foundation Establishment stage, was watching in front and might sense his spiritual sense.

Without using his spiritual sense and not being fully focused at the start, Chu Ning's spiritual power fluctuated slightly halfway through drawing the first talisman paper.

Unsurprisingly, this talisman paper self-ignited and turned to ashes.

While Chu Ning "failed" on his first attempt, the other twenty-odd people also gradually failed one after another.

Chu Ning's gaze unintentionally glanced around, observing...

Everyone's performance was within his sight, but his heart remained unperturbed.

"After all, we're all novices, plus with so many people around. It's very difficult to immediately enter the optimal state for talisman drawing, so making mistakes is normal."

Thinking this way, Chu Ning slightly adjusted his state, dipped his brush in ink, and began drawing on the second talisman paper.

This time, Chu Ning held nothing back. Although he didn't deliberately sense with his spiritual awareness, his entire mind was fully immersed in the process.

As such, this talisman was also successfully produced as naturally as a river flowing into a channel.

Chu Ning's successful talisman drawing naturally did not escape He Changyu's perception.

Although Chu Ning was not the only one who succeeded, He Changyu's gaze still turned towards him first.

At this moment, Chu Ning also revealed a hint of joy at the appropriate time.

Just then, some other people also succeeded in drawing talismans, and He Changyu's gaze shifted away.

For the second talisman paper, seven people succeeded.

This undoubtedly caused the remaining twenty people to feel immense pressure, with looks of anxiety appearing on many faces.

The result was that for the third talisman paper, many people still failed.

On Chu Ning's side, after finishing the second talisman paper, he was in an "excellent state."

He even succeeded with the third talisman paper, becoming one of only two disciples to complete two talisman papers.

For the subsequent few talisman papers, Chu Ning controlled the pace.

He succeeded in drawing 4 out of the first 9 talisman papers.

Among them, there were 3 that he truly failed to produce, and 1 that Chu Ning intentionally failed.

Nevertheless, Chu Ning was still one of only two disciples to successfully produce 4 talisman papers.

When everyone picked up the last talisman paper, He Changyu's gaze swept towards the two of them.

The next moment, Chu Ning suddenly sensed a very familiar feeling.

Spiritual awareness!

Chu Ning's heart jolted.

He didn't expect that at the last moment, He Changyu would release his spiritual awareness to sense his talisman drawing.

Having cultivated spiritual awareness before, Chu Ning was very clear.

This type of sensing was extremely sensitive and could detect even the slightest fluctuations in his spiritual power.

This undoubtedly put Chu Ning in a difficult position.

According to Chu Ning's previous plan, he aimed to first ensure completing 4 talisman papers out of the first 9.

This way, he had full confidence in ranking in the top ten and near the front.

After all, a 40% success rate was already very high for a novice.

Moreover, Chu Ning had just seen that another person also completed 4 talisman papers.

So, as long as he failed the last one, he could rank near the front without seeming too conspicuous.

But now, after discovering He Changyu's spiritual awareness sensing him, he hesitated.

Although talisman drawing could indeed be affected by the slightest fluctuations in spiritual power, Chu Ning was unsure.

If he intentionally caused fluctuations in his spiritual power, would He Changyu, a Foundation Establishment cultivator, be able to detect it?

"Forget it, let nature take its course," Chu Ning finally muttered to himself.

He decided to let the last talisman paper happen naturally and leave its success or failure to chance.

Because Chu Ning had paused to ponder for a bit earlier, he started this final talisman paper slightly later than the other person who had also completed 4.

So, when drawing the talisman, he didn't notice that the other person's talisman paper had spontaneously combusted halfway through, signaling failure.

All of He Changyu's spiritual awareness was now focused on Chu Ning.

At this time, Chu Ning was drawing naturally without any deliberate actions, allowing his state to perform normally.

"......"

When the azure light flashed once more and another successfully drawn Wood Talisman appeared on the table before him, Chu Ning couldn't help but fall silent with a slightly dazed expression.

"Truly, man cannot plan as well as heaven!"

This final talisman paper had also succeeded.

And at this moment, He Changyu's gaze towards Chu Ning was exceptionally bright.

Because Chu Ning had succeeded in drawing 5 out of the 10 talisman papers, achieving a 50% success rate!
