Ling Nong heard these words and his face also revealed a hint of perplexity.

Upon hearing this, Chu Ning couldn't help but shake his head inwardly. This cultivator at the third level of qi refining seemed to be already struggling with growing spiritual rice. Chu Ning nodded without saying much more. He took out a small handful of purple yam fruit seeds from his storage pouch, as well as two spirit stones and a long-lasting talisman, placing them on the counter.

The middle-aged spirit farmer didn't hesitate and immediately put down the spiritual rice he had just mentioned, as well as the spiritual lingfruit seeds in his other hand. He then quickly grabbed the items Chu Ning had taken out, as if afraid that Chu Ning would change his mind.

Seeing this, Chu Ning smiled and collected the spiritual rice and spiritual lingfruit seeds.

In this transaction, Chu Ning didn't make a profit. In fact, in terms of value, what he gave out was slightly higher.

If valued in spirit stones, the spiritual lingfruit would probably be worth twice as much as the purple yam fruit.

However, because the spiritual lingfruit was more difficult to cultivate and took longer, ordinary cultivators could only grow one season of spiritual lingfruit for every two seasons of purple yam fruit they grew.

So in terms of income, the difference between the two was not that great.

As for Chu Ning, there wasn't much difference in the difficulty of cultivating either of them.

The reason he wanted the other party's spiritual lingfruit seeds was entirely because the spiritual lingfruit fit his plans for the back courtyard.

The spiritual lingfruit grew on vines, which was very suitable for practicing the Thorny Vine Technique, even better than iron vines.

Chu Ning had always felt that the binding ability of the Thorny Vine Technique was quite good. It had been of great help when he fought Qi Chongmao in the past.

Although the Thorny Vine Technique was a low-grade elementary spell, the damage it inflicted after binding was greatly related to the strength of the spiritual plant itself.

At the same time, the spiritual lingfruit absorbed spiritual energy better than even the Ink Bamboo or the Purple Crystal Bamboo.

It was also very suitable for assisting in the cultivation of the Evergreen Wood Technique by absorbing spiritual energy from the Green Wood Longevity Art.

It was for these two reasons that Chu Ning chose to exchange for the spiritual lingfruit seeds.

The middle-aged spirit farmer had almost come from the end of the market street, and after he left, there were hardly any people left on the lively street.

After confirming that the market had finally dispersed, Chu Ning closed his shop and took the spiritual lingfruit seeds to the back courtyard.

The spiritual lingfruit's growth period for flowering and fruiting was longer than that of the iron vine, but not longer in terms of growing its vines.

The real difficulty in the early stages was catalyzing its growth.

Without reaching the instantaneous level of catalysis spells, it would be difficult to make the seeds sprout and grow.

Of course, this was not a problem for Chu Ning.

Chu Ning cast three consecutive instantaneous catalysis spells, and the spiritual lingfruit seeds began to sprout.

Then, Chu Ning followed up with the Evergreen Wood Technique, and the vines also started to grow slightly.

Chu Ning repeated this process, continuously catalyzing the growth of the spiritual lingfruit.

After planting the spiritual lingfruit vines around the perimeters of the two back courtyards, Chu Ning used his Blade Aura Technique to carve out a passageway in the connecting wall for people to pass through.

Only then did he nod in satisfaction.

There was still room to plant other things in these two courtyards.

Chu Ning had already made plans in his mind.
