In the following period of time, the two neighbors Luo Hongping and Lu Yunfang did not ask Chu Ning about Qi Chongmao again.

Moreover, Chu Ning did not feel the sense of being watched like when Qi Chongmao lived next door before.

This indicated that either the two felt there was nothing noteworthy about themselves, or although Qi Chongmao had cultivated the Refining Spirit Technique, the two had not cultivated it.

Chu Ning was more inclined towards the latter reason, because when he previously cultivated the Refining Spirit Technique, his spiritual sense had clearly extended to their residence. But the two seemed to have not noticed it.

However, regardless of which situation it was, Chu Ning did not let his guard down because of this.

During this period, Chu Ning rarely went out, spending almost every day going back and forth between the spirit field and the courtyard.

Even when sleeping at night, Chu Ning still placed a few talisman papers and that black wooden sword beside him as usual. The talisman papers allowed him to cast spells immediately, while this black wooden sword, although he could only control it in a straight line, could still block some attacks.

Moreover, except for sleeping at night, most of the time he stayed in the spirit field where the black spirit bamboo was planted. In the spirit field, he practiced the Green Wood Vernal Technique by using the black spirit bamboo to lay a solid foundation for refining qi.

At the same time, he did not neglect the Nine Revolutions Body Refining Technique and the Refining Spirit Technique either.

The cultivation of the Nine Revolutions Body Refining Technique was normal, but the cultivation of the Refining Spirit Technique surprised Chu Ning quite a bit.

Before condensing the spirit sense droplet, even though Chu Ning's innate spirit sense was powerful and quite suitable for cultivation, and he practiced diligently every day, it would take two days for his proficiency to increase by just 1 point. It was the slowest progressing technique since Chu Ning officially began cultivating.

Chu Ning had thought it was unlikely for this secret technique to take effect in the short term.

But after that day, when Chu Ning practiced it once in the morning and once in the evening, he could increase his proficiency by 1 point each day.

Moreover, after resting at night, he felt more energetic and vigorous, not at all like being in a relatively tense state of mind.

The effects of the Refining Spirit Technique were so good that it greatly exceeded Chu Ning's expectations.

As for spell cultivation, Chu Ning did not put much effort into it.

After realizing it was very difficult to make progress in cultivating the Golden Sword Technique and that the cost-effectiveness was too low, Chu Ning had completely given up on it.

However, during this period, after demonstrating the effects of his Thorny Vine Technique in the Practice Room, he learned another initial mid-grade offensive wood-type spell called the Wood Thorn Technique from the practice disciples.

It could use spiritual power to form wooden thorns from the ground to attack the opponent. If used in conjunction with the Thorny Vine Technique, the effects would be quite good.

However, Chu Ning still felt that the offensive power was a bit weak for now.

At the same time, the voice transmission from Luo Hongping and Lu Yunfang a few days ago also prompted Chu Ning to actively learn the Voice Transmission Technique.

As for some other basic wood-type spells, Chu Ning had basically mastered them.

Moreover, most of them had basically become innate spirit techniques that could be cast instantly, so he didn't need to cultivate them much.

In his spare time, Chu Ning also practiced the Fireball Technique more.

Although he didn't have the enhancement of a Yin Wood Spirit Body, with more practice, he became more proficient in it.

During these two months, Chu Ning basically didn't go to the market town at all. It seemed that no one had inquired about him buying talisman paper for Mu Ling.

Even if someone did inquire, Chu Ning was prepared to make up an excuse that he had bought a lot at once previously.

Time passed quickly like this, and in the blink of an eye, more than two months had gone by. Chu Ning became busy again, because it was time for the seasonal harvest.

The purple knotweed matured earlier than the black spirit bamboo, so it had been harvested more than a month ago.

This spirit plant didn't need to be harvested like spirit rice where the grains had to be removed and then sun-dried into rice after reaping. The purple knotweed could be directly pulled out by the roots.

Since Chu Ning had no use for this purple knotweed himself...
