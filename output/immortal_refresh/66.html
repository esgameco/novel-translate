Upon returning to his own courtyard, Chu Ning couldn't wait to start taking things out from his storage bag.

Today's harvest was really huge.

A mid-grade talisman pen, basics of low-grade talisman making, high-grade talismans, the Qingmu Sword Manual...

Chu Ning looked at this pile of good stuff on the table, and after feasting his eyes on them, he thought for a moment and put away all the other items, leaving only the jade slip on the basics of low-grade talisman making.

As for the Qingmu Sword Manual, it would be better to try it out after moving to the outer sect quarters.

It was more important to first take a look at these basics of talisman making to improve his talisman crafting skills.

After all, to be honest, he was able to obtain so many benefits today thanks to revealing his decent innate talent for talisman making.

The contents of this jade slip on the basics of low-grade talisman making were written in great detail. As Chu Ning read through it, he couldn't help being drawn in.

Previously when he made the Wood Escape Talisman, he relied more on his intuition and the innate talent of his talisman spirit body.

But he didn't really understand some of the basic knowledge and principles behind talisman making.

For example, even the most basic grading of talismans was something Chu Ning only had a vague understanding of before.

According to the jade slip, talismans are graded as low-grade, mid-grade, high-grade, and top-grade.

Each grade is further divided into low-rank, mid-rank, and high-rank, and talismans of different grades require different types of talisman paper.

The regular talisman paper Chu Ning used before could only be used to make low-grade talismans.

To make mid-grade and high-grade talismans, one would need mid-grade talisman paper and high-grade talisman paper respectively.

Some special talismans even required using spirit plants or demon beast hides to draw them.

Top-grade talismans all required using special demon beast hides.

There were also corresponding grades for talisman masters - those who could make talismans of a certain grade with a success rate of over 50% were called low-grade, mid-grade, high-grade, and top-grade talisman masters.

Of course, since there was a big difference between the low, mid, and high ranks within the low-grade talismans, the cultivation world also used the terms first-grade, second-grade, and third-grade talisman masters to distinguish the levels of low-grade talisman masters.

These were just introductions to the basic knowledge of talismans. The contents of this jade slip went far beyond that.

It included a more detailed systematic introduction to talismans, and as Liao Yunming said, it also contained many insights and experiences.

Chu Ning gained a lot from reading it.

For half a day, Chu Ning spent his time studying the basic knowledge in this jade slip.

He didn't even look at the few talisman making methods attached after that.

Only after finishing all the knowledge introductions did Chu Ning slowly put down the jade slip and sink into contemplation, digesting and comprehending the knowledge.

With his innate talent as a talisman spirit body, his ability to comprehend talisman making was naturally extremely strong.

So he was able to digest this knowledge very quickly.

After a long while, Chu Ning finally came out of his contemplation, a smile appearing on his face.

He didn't choose to continue reading the few talisman making methods in the jade slip.

Instead, he took out the talisman pen and talisman ink he had before, along with a large stack of blank talisman paper, from his storage bag.

"Let me first use the Wood Escape Talisman to verify what I've comprehended."

Saying this softly, Chu Ning picked up the talisman pen and slowly infused it with spiritual power.

Dipping the ink, putting down the brush, moving it around, and lifting the brush, Chu Ning completed the making of a Wood Escape Talisman in one go.

A flash of green light, and the talisman was successfully made as expected.

Immediately after, Chu Ning started drawing talisman after talisman non-stop.

After making 20 talismans this way, Chu Ning put down this pen and took out the mid-grade talisman pen gifted by Liao Yunming from his storage bag.

The infusion of spiritual power was extremely smooth - Chu Ning felt that as soon as he infused his spiritual power, it went straight to the pen tip.

"No wonder it's a mid-grade talisman pen!"

When Chu Ning enveloped it with his spiritual sense, he also felt it was much smoother and easier.

With a wave of the brush, it was as if aided by divine forces. In just a short while, Chu Ning drew another 20 talismans.

"Talisman Source Spirit Body!"

Chu Ning silently chanted these four words in his heart, once again marveling at the powerful innate talents of the spirit body.

Although he had already recognized the formidable talents of the Tiger Wood Spirit Body when he previously cultivated the Green Wood Everspring Skill.

Picking up the jade slip again, Chu Ning began reading the talismans that came with it.

The talismans built into this jade slip were all low-grade beginner talismans.

There were offensive ones like the Blazing Fire Talisman and Profound Ice Talisman, defensive ones like the Earth Armor Talisman, and healing ones like the Rejuvenation Talisman.

Among them, there was even one related to the cultivation of spiritual plants.

Used to enhance the quality of spiritual plants, it was called the Evergreen Talisman.

At this point, Chu Ning didn't pick and choose, comprehending the talisman-making methods of each talisman one by one before immediately starting to make them.

For three days, Chu Ning confined himself completely in the courtyard.

He didn't go out the main gate or the side gate, single-mindedly making talismans.

Taking the low-grade beginner talismans from the basic talisman jade slip, he constantly experimented with them.

When tired, he would cultivate. When his state recovered, he would make talismans.

Chu Ning once again entered the state of disregarding sleep and food like a few days ago.

It also allowed Chu Ning to somewhat experience the kind of crazed talisman-making state that Mu Ling had.

This stuff is really addictive.

In three days, Chu Ning learned all five kinds of talismans.

Although the success rate was only 40%, Chu Ning had absolute confidence.

Just give him about half a month more, and he could definitely raise the success rate of making all five talismans above 60%!

This undoubtedly made Chu Ning marvel again at the might of the Talisman Source Spirit Body.

On the fourth day at dawn, after Chu Ning got up and as usual cultivated the Spirit Refining Technique, Green Wood Everspring Skill, and Nine Revolutions Body Refining Technique.

He didn't choose to make talismans, but instead came to the courtyard.

"Previously I didn't really want to reveal too many of my capabilities in front of the neighbors Lu Yunfang and Luo Hongping.

I still haven't figured out their relationship with Qi Chongmao, but I always felt the two seemed to pose a threat to me.

But now, they have moved to the C District to live in the past couple days.

I don't need to have any reservations, it's time to study this Azure Wood Sword Technique."

Chu Ning muttered to himself, then casually took out a jade slip from his storage pouch.

It was the Azure Wood Sword Technique that He Changyou had given him before.

At that time, He Changyou had specifically mentioned that this skill was very suitable for someone who had cultivated the Green Wood Vernal Skill to practice.

This piqued Chu Ning's curiosity.

Picking up the jade slip, Chu Ning examined its contents.

It was then that he realized, although this Azure Wood Sword Technique was named a sword technique, it did not actually require using a sword.

It was just that the attacks released by this skill resembled swords, hence the name.

And after Chu Ning finished reading it, he finally understood.

Why He Changyou said this skill was very suitable for someone who had cultivated the Green Wood Vernal Skill.

Because the principle behind the Azure Wood Sword Technique had some similarities with the Green Wood Vernal Skill.

The Green Wood Vernal Skill acted on spiritual plants, helping them condense and absorb the spiritual qi of heaven and earth.

While the Azure Wood Sword Technique's skill, if struck on a cultivator's body, had the effect of extracting their spiritual qi and power.

Not only did it work on cultivators' bodies, but even if used on protective force fields,

It could also attract the spiritual qi within, thereby augmenting itself and increasing its attack power.
