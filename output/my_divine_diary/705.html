Chapter 705

Here is my translation of the excerpt from the Chinese novel:

Just as Suhao was about to start making the Seeker Ring, the captain came running through the transit ball to find Suhao.

This year the captain had exercised less and socialized more, his belly protruding slightly despite the shaping effects of the enhancer, no longer looking like the tough middle-aged captain but more like a portly middle-aged uncle.

Suhao paused his work and asked, "What's going on? Why the rush?"

The captain said with some resignation, "Your niece Yonghua has been asking around about your whereabouts, suspecting you've been kidnapped. It's even trending on the news now. Take a look yourself!"

The captain showed Suhao his personal terminal and opened a web browser.

They saw the top trending topic was: 'Niece of superhero Xu Shiqiang' questions whether Xu Shiqiang has been treated unfairly. Is this really the case? How should we view this issue?

Suhao laughed, "What does she want to find me for?"

The captain shrugged, "I don't know. She says she wants to learn knowledge from you, to become a useful person in the future. But her grades are just average...

Maybe she just wants to get closer to her famous uncle. After all, you great Xu Shiqiang only go home once a year. Or maybe she simply wants to fulfill a young girl's hero fantasy, given your identity... To be honest, if I were your relative, I might fanboy harder than your niece."

Suhao laughed, "You could look up our ancestry, we might be blood-related going back millions of years."

The captain said, "Then we must be relatives for sure..."

Suhao said indifferently, "Let her come find me if she wants. It's not a big deal, we'll know once we ask her."

So what if her grades aren't good? As long as she's willing to study hard, she can learn something. He didn't mind teaching this niece.

Moreover, didn't he, Suhao, used to be utterly mediocre long ago?

Suhao chuckled, "It's just about the Compound Eye Race getting our new technology, right? No problem, once I get some time, I'll research how the Compound Eyes obtain secret tech, and design countermeasures tailored to their abilities."

The unspoken second half was: "If I can't block them, then I'll just wipe out the entire Compound Eye Race."

The captain thought for a moment, "Okay, let her in, but I'll talk to her first."

Suhao nodded, he had no objections.

Ever since finding a way to evade warp tracking, time was no longer so urgent for him, and his attitude towards things had subtly changed, becoming more casual.

It was like before when he only had enough money for one day's meal and had to think about how to stretch it for a few more days.

Now as a multi-millionaire, he didn't worry about food and drink, and could take it easy.

...

Speaking of the Compound Eye Race, one couldn't avoid discussing the racial distribution in the Cloud Ring System.

Human astronomers hypothesized:

The Cloud Ring System was very special in the universe. Under the complex interplay of cosmic forces, miraculously, there was frequent material exchange between planets.

This might explain why the Cloud Ring had so many life-bearing planets and intelligent races.

They guessed that in the distant past, life emerged on one planet that prospered, but then suffered some catastrophe that shattered it into fragments.

These fragments carried massive amounts of life information that spread to various planets in the Cloud Ring, creating the vibrant scene of life there.

Of course, among so many life-bearing planets, only a small proportion could give rise to intelligence and civilization. But that small proportion across an entire system was still a large number.

Unfortunately, among these intelligent races, the Eight-Handed Race with their nine brains and exceptional intelligence developed first.

Then the disaster for the other races in the Cloud Ring began.

Under the Eight-Handed's "survival of the smartest, Eight-Handed supremacy" policy, countless intelligent races suffered devastating blows and gradually disappeared from cosmic history.

Just as the Eight-Handed were about to dominate the Cloud Ring, humans had only just gone through the electrical revolution and were still very weak - they should have been doomed to the same fate of extinction.

However, among the Cloud Ring's intelligent races, there was one oddball - the Compound Eye Race.

Their visual system was completely different from humans, Eight-Handed and most other races. They could somehow perceive the "second structural feature" of planets through special signals.

Then the Compound Eyes used their special eyes to peer across light years of distance and saw the Eight-Handed's technology...

Without the Eight-Handed knowing, they stole a lot of their high-tech.

Unfortunately, while the Compound Eyes' eyes were capable, their technological intelligence was lacking, so they couldn't understand most of the tech they stole!

Can you imagine having a treasure trove of tech but not understanding any of it? Ask the Compound Eyes.

When the Compound Eyes had nothing better to do, they liked randomly peering into space.

They watched helplessly as the Eight-Handed fleet exterminated race after race, trembling as they awaited the fleet's arrival at their own homeworld to destroy them too.

Then one smart Compound Eye suggested: "We may not understand the tech, but we can find other races who do. We'll steal the tech, they'll research it, and together we can resist the Eight-Handed's extermination."

Coincidentally, the Compound Eyes managed to find humans on Earth before the Eight-Handed, using their crude communication devices to make contact.

After a few hundred years of such efforts, humans finally believed the Compound Eyes' "nonsense" - because using the tech the Compound Eyes provided, humans achieved warp travel, only for their first ramshackle ship to be blasted apart by the Eight-Handed.

Humans fell into panic; civil war broke out; a unified alliance formed; a technological explosion occurred; humans succeeded in cloaking Earth; humans turned the tables and subjugated the Compound Eyes...

Then the Compound Eyes threatened humans: share all tech and teach us, or we'll reveal Earth's location to the Eight-Handed for mutual destruction.

So the Compound Eyes became the masters humans had to worship!

What could they do? They couldn't just say outright: "You Compound Eyes are too dumb, we can't teach you, just give up!"

The Compound Eyes would probably have a meltdown and betray the humans, ruining everything.

So humans came up with delaying tactics - teaching in full force outwardly, but making excuses when the Compound Eyes couldn't learn, like "adults' mindsets are set, better to start from childhood" or "language translation is a huge issue, the Compound Eyes should all learn human languages" or "human learning methods don't suit Compound Eyes, we're researching methods tailored to you - after all, there are no unteachable students, only bad teachers..."

What else could they do if the Compound Eyes couldn't learn? They just hoped the Compound Eyes would stay calm and not go to extremes - survival was paramount.

The Compound Eyes' survival intelligence was high, but their technological intelligence really wasn't...

While teaching, humans directly provided the Compound Eyes with tech products, teaching them hands-on how to use everything, helping them cloak their homeworld and build a fleet to jointly resist the Eight-Handed.

Of course, this was all done remotely - the Compound Eyes adamantly refused to let any humans near their homeworld.

For humans, it was great to have someone distracting the Eight-Handed's attention!

For the Compound Eyes, it was great to have help building powerful ships and weapons!

However, the Compound Eyes ultimately weren't on the same side as humans - different tribes have different minds.

Their current cooperation was only due to the mutual Eight-Handed threat. Once the Eight-Handed weakened enough, the Compound Eyes would surely betray humans, waiting for both sides to destroy each other so they could profit.

So from start to finish, humans remained wary of the Compound Eyes, hoping to eventually break free of their shackles completely.

...

As for Suhao...

He was extremely curious about the Compound Eye Race.

This special race could clearly perceive half the Cloud Ring system and, crucially, steal the Eight-Handed's technical data from afar?

In a sense, the Compound Eyes' vision was even more powerful than his own "Mind's Eye" - so powerful that Suhao could barely stop himself from drooling with excitement.

The "Mind's Eye" he created in his previous life was a stopgap measure; installing a "Seeker Ring" in his brain was ultimately inconvenient.

Perhaps by unraveling the Compound Eyes' life secrets, he could not only gain their abilities, but evolve the "Mind's Eye" into a physical capability, maybe even grow it directly on his psychic power!

Whenever he thought of this, Suhao could hardly contain his urge to research the Compound Eyes immediately.

Unfortunately, while humans had a rough guess of the Compound Eyes' homeworld location, they hadn't confirmed it, so Suhao had no way to obtain Compound Eye specimens.

Most bizarrely, humans only knew their name "Compound Eye Race" - they didn't even know what the Compound Eyes looked like, only able to imagine based on their custom ship designs.

Flies? Dragonflies? Bees? Spiders? Eyeballs with limbs?

However, Suhao believed he would have answers soon.

Because he planned to thoroughly master all human knowledge, complete the necessary experiments, and then personally visit the Compound Eyes' homeworld...
