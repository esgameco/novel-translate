Chapter 781

Here is my translation of the excerpt from the Chinese novel:

Another fifty years passed, and Feng Cheng sent a message again: "Master Wei, Master Ya Shan, I seem to have discovered another life-bearing planet."

This time, not only was Ya Shan stunned, but even Su Hao was very surprised as he looked at Feng Cheng.

Ya Shan couldn't help but ask: "Feng Cheng, do you have some secret exploration method? Why is it always you who finds them? Two or three times is understandable, but four or five times in a row!"

Su Hao also nodded and said: "Feng Cheng, come to my lab sometime and let me study whether you have some kind of invincible cosmic emperor's luck!"

Ya Shan: "I think we really need to dissect you."

Feng Cheng immediately waved his hand and said: "What luck? Master Wei is right, this is completely a matter of probability!"

Ya Shan found this reasonable, and after pondering for a moment said: "Or should we switch back?"

Feng Cheng immediately nodded: "Switch, we must switch. Next time Master Ya Shan will definitely discover the life-bearing planet first!"

The three of them began to explore this planet.

There was no intelligent life...

However, Su Hao discovered a few special animals on this planet.

The closer ones could accurately locate their nests from hundreds of kilometers away, while the farther ones could even locate globally.

After careful observation and perception, Su Hao confirmed it was different from the normal positive and negative magnetic fields of a planet.

"Could it be some kind of planetary force field I've never seen before?"

After five years of investigation, Su Hao discovered two 'seeker' substances, named 'Seeker 195' and 'Seeker 196', and through these two seekers, discovered two kinds of planetary force fields.

Three years later, referring to those special animals and using 'Seeker 196', Su Hao successfully created a 'spiritual eye'.

It was called the 'Planetary Spiritual Eye'.

Its effect was extremely powerful - it could directly observe the entire appearance of a planet, enveloping the whole planet in perception like a divine consciousness.

However, its precision was low. It could accurately locate a specific location, but could not clearly perceive the objects on it, not even distinguishing human shapes.

But it still made Su Hao very excited, as this was his first ability to directly observe an entire planet.

This ability was different from what he had seen in the 'Planetary Network' - the planets seen there were essentially just from a 'perspective' view.

Whereas the Planetary Spiritual Eye could envelop the entire planet like a divine consciousness - they were not the same concept.

...

Another hundred years passed.

Su Hao finally discovered a life-bearing planet before Feng Cheng.

This relieved him, confirming that it had nothing to do with luck, and was related to probability...

After researching it, Su Hao obtained a new neural system structure from the large organisms on this planet that was superior to the human neural structure.

...

Thirty years later, Feng Cheng discovered a life-bearing planet.

On it, Su Hao obtained an optimized energy structure, greatly enhancing the power of his spirit force grid.

A hundred years later, Su Hao discovered a life-bearing planet.

And on this planet, he obtained a special protein cell membrane structure, which gave rise to a strange idea in his mind: Perhaps I could replace all the cell membranes in my body with spirit force - wouldn't that allow me to access more spirit force with my body?

Let me try it.

Failure, failure, failure...

Su Hao thought to himself: "Every time it's a failed success - the hardest part is distinguishing beneficial substances from viruses. I'll shelve this project for now and wait until I gain more knowledge in the future."

Another seventy years later, Feng Cheng discovered a life-bearing planet.

...

Ya Shan was about to cry.

"Master Wei, Feng Cheng, can you two teach me how to find life-bearing planets!"

Su Hao consoled him: "Keep going Ya Shan, I believe the next planet will be found by you."

Feng Cheng also encouraged him: "That's right Master Ya Shan, this is simply a matter of probability. Perhaps the direction you've been exploring just doesn't happen to have life-bearing planets."

Ya Shan: "But I've switched with you many times, and every time after switching it's you who finds one. How do you explain that?"

Feng Cheng: "Coincidence...so, Master Ya Shan, do you want to switch this time or not?"

Ya Shan immediately fell into an extremely conflicted state, like guessing heads or tails on a coin flip - every time he guessed heads, it came up tails, and every time he guessed tails, it came up heads. He was utterly perplexed.

Finally, Ya Shan waved his hand decisively: "No switching. I don't believe I can't find one."

After a moment, Ya Shan asked: "Master Wei, Feng Cheng, when you two are searching, do you have an expressionless face or a slight smile? I feel like maybe my expression is wrong."

Feng Cheng quipped: "Last time you said it was your posture that was wrong..."

Time flew by rapidly, and five thousand years passed.

The three of them explored countless galaxies, and discovered all kinds of life-bearing planets in many galaxies, including many that had developed civilizations.

At the same time, on these planets they harvested various strange little abilities and knowledge to continuously optimize their own ability systems.

Most importantly, in the Billiard Dimension, Su Hao recorded a vast amount of information - any strange phenomena or substances were crammed into it without exception.

For him, many phenomena and substances he couldn't understand now might suddenly make sense in the future.

Better to record something wrong than to miss anything.

Of course, during these thousands of years, most of the time was spent on the journey of exploration itself, with not much time actually spent stopping to conduct research.

After all, although they explored quite a few life-bearing planets over these years, there were not many useful findings.

Most crucially, among all these civilized planets, there was not a single humanoid intelligent species...

Their appearances were all bizarre creatures - what were essentially large-headed aquatic tribes, bird tribes, small underground sprites, tentacle monsters...

The civilizations developed by such intelligent species looked simply indescribable to the human Su Hao...

Feng Cheng kept exclaiming in amazement: "I never imagined these weird things could also develop intelligence and civilization. I always thought only humans had a greater probability of developing civilization - I considered the Octopus Tribe an exception. Who could have guessed that all kinds of different species in the universe could develop intelligence?"

Since Ya Shan found far fewer life-bearing planets than Feng Cheng, he had long been depressed about it, so he took this opportunity to disdainfully glance at Feng Cheng.

Feng Cheng didn't seem at all like someone who had lived for thousands of years...

Perhaps without the scheming and interpersonal dealings, time alone does not age one's mentality. Or perhaps mentality and physical state are closely linked.

In any case, at over six thousand years old, Su Hao and the others were still able to maintain a youthful and vigorous mentality.

As for the time it took them to find life-bearing planets, there was no set pattern.

The shortest was ten years to find one, the longest was five hundred years.

It was completely a matter of probability.

And in Su Hao's view, there was no discernible pattern to the birth of life-bearing planets.

If one had to say the emergence of life was related to something, then it would have to be 'coincidence'.

Over these years, they discovered innumerable planets suitable for the emergence and proliferation of life, but so many habitable planets were barren.

Su Hao even felt that many planets didn't need to be terraformed - if a large chunk of soil containing plants was transplanted from other life-bearing planets, countless years later, those planets would inevitably develop rich species too.

At the same time, a plan gradually took shape in Su Hao's mind: It was such a pity that these habitable planets had no life flourishing on them. Perhaps he could sow the seeds of life and wait for them to sprout and grow in the future.

Sow a seed in spring, reap a harvest in autumn.

Of course, by 'seeds of life' he didn't mean transplanting organisms from other planets, but rather designing a 'life form' according to his own imagination and then releasing it onto planets to observe its evolutionary state.

But the timing wasn't ripe yet.

"I can feel my physical body gradually aging. Perhaps if I persevere for another two thousand years, it will completely decay and I'll die. Even if I sow the seeds of life now, I likely won't live to see the harvest."

Eight thousand years was indeed an extremely long lifespan...

But on the cosmic scale, it still seemed too short.

That day, Su Hao called Ya Shan and Feng Cheng back.

Su Hao said: "We've been exploring outwards for six thousand years now. I don't know how human civilization in the Cloud Ring System has developed, so I plan to go back and take a look. Do you two want to come along?"

In six thousand years, the three had explored who knows how many galaxies, yet the galaxies they traveled through were just a tiny speck in the observable universe...

Even if they continued outwards for the remaining two thousand years, they couldn't go much further.

The exploration of the cosmos had only just begun.

When Ya Shan and Feng Cheng heard this, they immediately became excited.

Finally, they could return to human society and indulge for a while.

After so many years, even opening treasure chests would get stale.
